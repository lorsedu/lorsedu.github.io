15. The temperature at carmona, cavite at 1:00PM was 32°C. Between 1:00PM and 5:00PM, the temperature dropped 3.2°C. What is the temperature at 5:00PM?​


Topic: PREDICTION

======================================================

The question:

If the tempereature was 32°C at 1:00 PM, and it dropped 3.2°C between 1:00-5:00 PM, then what will the temperature be at 5:00 PM?

The solution:

The first step is to find the temperature between the times of 1:00 and 5:00 PM.

The temperature dropped 3.2°C at that same time. The temperature at 1:00 PM was 32°C. The tempeature between 1 and 5 PM is 32°C - 3.2°C, which is 28.8°C.

Now the drop would most probably also be 3.2°C, and the temperature at 5:00 PM will most probably be the temeprature earlier (28.8°C) minus 3.2°C, which is 25.6°C.

The answer:

The temperature at 5 PM will be 25.6°C.

= 300 km + 200 km

= 500 km.

Epicenter of an earthquake in Carmona is 500 km.

Scientists use triangulation to define the epicenter of an earthquake. When seismic data is compiled from at least three different locations, it can be used to determine the epicenter by where it intersects. Every earthquake is recorded on numerous seismographs placed in different directions.

Each seismograph records the times when the first (P waves) and second (S waves) seismic waves start.

From that information, scientists can define how fast the waves are traveling. Knowing this assists them to calculate the distance from the epicenter to each seismograph.

To describe the direction each wave traveled, scientists draw circles around the seismograph locations. The radius of each circle is equivalent to the known distance to the epicenter. Where these three spheres intersect is the epicenter.

26. draw the location of the epicenter of an earthquake in Carmona, Silang, GMA Cavite if the hypothetical distance of epicenter in Carmona is 300 Km, Silang is 200 lkm, and GMA is 200km​ paturo po^_^.​


This method is used by geologists and seismologists called the triangulation method to determine the location of the epicenter. For the triangulation method to work we need three locations along with their hypothetical distance from the epicenter.

You will need:

A compassA pencilDifferent colored pensA copy of the map

Steps in doing the triangulation method:

From the map find the location labeled Carmona.this is where you will put the needle of your compass. Draw at least three circles of increasing size. A pencil would do but I recommend using a different colored pen for each location.Repeat steps 1 to 3 for the location Silang and GMA.

Note: Since there is no numbered scale provided, we will draw different sizes of circles but regarding the measurement of the circles, Silang and GMA’s circles must be roughly the same size and Carmona must have the biggest circle of them all. This is because of the distance provided from the epicenter.

When you’re done drawing the circles, there should be a point in the circles where all three different circles centered in three different locations would intersect. The point where they intersect is the epicenter of the earthquake.
