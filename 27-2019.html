20. A freshly isolated sample of a radioisotope was found to have an activity of 6.67 x 10^5 disintegrations per minute at 4:30 pm on September 22, 2019, and at 1:20 pm on September 27, 2019, the activity was redetermined and found to be 2.88 x 10^3 disintegrations per minute. Calculate the half-life of the radioisotope in hrs.


Answer:

69.6 hours

Explanation:

To calculate the half-life of a radioisotope, we need to use the equation t1/2 = 0.693 * (t1/A1)/(t2/A2), where t1/2 is the half-life, t1 is the time of the first measurement, A1 is the activity at time t1, t2 is the time of the second measurement, and A2 is the activity at time t2. In this case, t1 is the time of the first measurement (4:30 pm on September 22, 2019), A1 is the activity at t1 (6.67 x 10^5 disintegrations per minute), t2 is the time of the second measurement (1:20 pm on September 27, 2019), and A2 is the activity at t2 (2.88 x 10^3 disintegrations per minute). Plugging these values into the equation, we get t1/2 = 0.693 * (5.6 days / 6.67 x 10^5 disintegrations per minute) / (5.2 days / 2.88 x 10^3 disintegrations per minute) = 0.693 * 0.84 days / 0.18 days = 2.9 days. Therefore, the half-life of the radioisotope is 2.9 days, or 69.6 hours.
