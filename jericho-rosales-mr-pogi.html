It is density dependent and the limiting factor is emigration. A factor that regulates a population’s growth and is influenced by population density, is called density-dependent limiting factor. If the population’s density does not directly influence changes in population’s growth, then it is called a density-independent limiting factor. Density-dependent limiting factors come into play when a population reaches a certain number of organisms. For example, when a population reaches a certain size, there won’t be enough resources (food, shelter, water) for all of the organisms. This could cause the population to stop growing when it reaches the maximum number of organisms that can be supported, or “carried,” by the environment. This number is known as the population’s carrying capacity in a particular environment. Each population of organisms has a different carrying capacity, depending on the amount of resources available in the area in which it lives.

Answer:

14 years later

Step-by-step explanation:

First, let us set a table. Since the year wherein the age of Mrs. Rosales is equivalent to twice of her daughter's, let us set the year to x.

Present year X Year

Mrs Rosales. 50. 50+x

Eldest daughter 18. 18+x

So let us set this as an equation

50+x=2(18+x) *since age of mrs rosales will equivalent twice the amount of eldest daughter age

50+x=36+2x *distribute 2

14=x *combine like terms

and there u have it, in 14 years she will be twice her daughters age. lets check

50+14 =2(18+14)

64=36+28

64=64

When will be the mother be twiceas old as her eldest daughter?

No. of years to make this statement true

given:
