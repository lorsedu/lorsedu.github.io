23. 26. A promoter of a show computed that if the price of ticket is P650, only 50 people might watch the show. If the price of the ticket is decreased by P50, the number of people that might watch the show can increase by 10. Which mathematical statement best represents the given situation? Hint: Revenue R(x) = (price per unit) (number of units produced or sold) A. Revenue (x) = (50 + 10x) (650 – 50x) B. Revenue (x) = (650 + 10x) (50 - 50x) C. Revenue (x) = (50-10x) (650 + 50x) D. Revenue (x) = (650 - 10x) (50 + 50x)​


Let x be the number of times the price was decreased by P50, then the new price is P650 - P50x and the number of people that might watch the show is 50 + 10x.

To find the revenue, we multiply the price per ticket by the number of tickets sold, which is (650 - 50x) for the original price and (650 - 50x)(50 + 10x) for the decreased price.

So the mathematical statement that represents the given situation is:

Revenue (x) = (650 - 50x)(50 + 10x)

Therefore, the correct answer is option C.
