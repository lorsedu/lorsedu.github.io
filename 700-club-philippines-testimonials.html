Answer:

Dead Stars" by Paz Marquez-Benitez is a short story about a love triangle between two men, Alfredo and Julio, and a woman, Esperanza. The story takes place in the Philippines in the 1920s and explores the theme of unrequited love and the consequences of societal expectations. Esperanza is torn between her love for the passionate and impulsive Julio and her duty to marry the more practical and successful Alfredo. The story ends with Esperanza choosing to marry Alfredo, but ultimately feeling a sense of emptiness and regret for not following her heart.

Explanation:

"Dead Stars" by Paz Marquez-Benitez is a short story about a man named Alfredo Salazar, who is torn between his love for his former sweetheart, Esperanza, and his current fiancée, Julia. The story takes place in the Philippines in the early 20th century and explores themes of love, longing, and the passage of time. It is considered one of the first modern Philippine short stories, and is notable for its use of symbolism and stream-of-consciousness narrative. The story was first published in the Philippines Free Press in 1925 and is considered a classic of Philippine literature.
