19. A deck of 54 cards has 2 jokers, and 13 cards of each of spades, hearts, clubs and diamonds. At least how many cards should be drawn at random so that there are at least 4 cards of the same suit? asap po


Answer:

15

Step-by-step explanation:

Consider the worst situation that we have 2 jokers and 3 cards from each suit to be  drawn.

There are in total 14 cards but still did not give us a suit with more than 4  cards.

And when one more card is drawn, we will have 4 cards for a certain suit,  by pigeon hole principle. Thus the answer is 15 cards.

Theoretical probability.

Suppose you have a standard deck of playing cards containing 52 cards. A standard deck has 4 suit (heart,club,diamond, and spade) each suit has an ace, a king, a jack, and a queen, and 9 numbers (2 to 10). hearts and diamonds are red cards while spades and clubs are black cards.

What we know: 52 total cards4 suits (heart,club,diamond, and spade)Each suit has 13 cardsEach suit has an ACE, a KING, a JACK, & a QUEENHearts & Diamonds are REDSpades & Clubs are BLACK

If a card is drawn, find the probability that it is:

A. a spade

There are 13 spades out of 52 cards

Hence, n(S)= 13/52 or 1/4

B. a red card

There are 13 Hearts & 13 Diamonds which are RED, out of 52 cards

(13 + 13 = 26)

Hence, n(S)= 26/52 or 1/2

C. a queen

Each suit has a queen whether it is a diamond, spade, heart or club. So there are a total of 4 queens out of 52 cards.

Hence, n(S)= 4/52 or 1/13

D. a black face card

There are 13 Spades & 13 Clubs which are BLACK, out of 52 cards. (13 +13=26)

Hence, n(S)= 26/52 or 1/2

E. a king or a queen

Just what I have said before, there are a total 4 queens so that means the same goes to the king whether it's a spade, heart, diamond, or club, there are a total of 4 kings.

Hence, n(S) = 8/52 or 2/13
