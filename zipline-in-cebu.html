5. Situation: A zipline is a very strong cable between two parallel points attached to it. Draw the zipline connected from a 20 foot tall tower to a 15 foot tower in a slightly inclined ground. The angles created by the line to the top of the poles are (2x+15) and (3x)". Find the measure of these angles.​


Answer:

Therefore, the measure of the two angles is 81° and 99°

Step-by-step explanation:

In this problem, we are tasked to find the measure of the angles and draw the problem.

(picture is attached)

As the figure shown, we can see two angles are adjacent angles which means they add up to 180°. Therefore,

[tex] \rm{(2x+15)+(3x)=180}[/tex]

Group like terms

[tex] \rm{2x+3x+15=180}\\\\\rm{5x = 165}\\\\\rm{x = \frac{165}{5}}\\\\\rm{x = 33} [/tex]

Now, that we find the value of x. We will solve for now the measure of the angles. To find the measure of the angles, simply substitute the value of x to the angles and simplify to get the measure of the angles.

[tex] \cdot \ \ \ 2x+15 = 2(33)+15=81 [/tex]

[tex] \cdot \ \ \ 3x = 3(33) = 99 [/tex]

Therefore, the measure of the two angles is 81° and 99°

24. Why do you fall in zipline upside-down?​


Answer:

Your acceleration due to gravity decreases.

Explanation:

Gravity is the essential force in bringing you down the zipline. Without it, when you step off that platform, you wouldn't be going anywhere. ... Gravity always pulls directly toward the ground, so when you're falling on an incline, your acceleration due to gravity decreases.
