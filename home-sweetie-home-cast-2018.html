NET LOSS

A negative net income indicates that a company made a loss rather than a profit during a given accounting period. While a company's sales may be positive, its expenses and other costs will have exceeded the money taken in as revenue.

NET INCOME

The net income can be positive or negative. If your revenues exceed your expenses, you will have a positive net income. If your expenses exceed your income, you will have a negative net income, also known as a net loss.

Net income is defined as gross profit less all other expenses and costs, as well as any other income and revenue sources not included in gross profit. Interest on debt, taxes, and operating expenses or overhead costs are some of the costs subtracted from gross to arrive at net income.

AVOID NET INCOME LOSS

If your company is already incurring net losses, take the following steps to avoid them.

Spend less money.Increase the company's sales.Consult an accountant or a business advisor.
