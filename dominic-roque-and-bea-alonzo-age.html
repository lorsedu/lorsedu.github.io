Problem:

Jisa's age is three years more than twice Bea's age. The sum of their ages is 69. How old is each?

Solution:

Bea's age = 2(Jisa's age) + 3

Jisa's age + Bea's age = 69

Let x be the Bea's age and let y be the Jisa's age.

Equation 1.

Jisa's age is three years more than twice Bea's age.

y = 2x + 3

Equation 2.

The sum of their ages is 69.

x + y = 69

Solve the problem.

x + (2x + 3) = 693x + 3 = 693x = 69 - 33x = 66/3x = 22

x + (2x + 3) = 69 by using substituting y = 2x + 3

y = 2x + 3y = 2(22) + 3y = 44 + 3y = 47

Answer:Thus, the Bea's age is 22 and Jisa's age is 47.
