The supply chain is often determined by the time it takes to respond to the market behavior. If it takes ages to produce and deliver the desired result to the customers, it is often branded as slow and archaic. However, there are various degrees of understanding, which can lead us to the right conclusion of deciding whether a supply chain is responsive or not. Some of the considerations we have to make include:

Which industry does the supply chain belong to: Certain industries like the pharmaceutical take a longer time to research and patent a product before manufacturing the same. The manufacturing process is also ridden with a number of challenges that are beyond the understanding of the common public. Some of these challenges include tracking counterfeit drugs in the chain, delay in information back and forth, traceability practices and more. While the same holds true for any supply chain, they are not DSCSA-regulated or are not governed by any government body.

How much time does it take to deliver the finished goods: If we take the example of the pharmaceutical industry, the turn around time of finished products is bound to take days because of the expanse of research and steps taken to deliver the right products. Production in the pharmaceutical industry happens in a phased manner, involving a lot of data sharing and tallying processes, which is unlike any other supply chain. So, it really makes no sense comparing the supply chain of industries like the pharma with any other supply chain.

The dynamism of the market: Unlike any other industry, the pharma industry is dynamic and agile in more than one way. There’s always something new about one medicine or the other Research might disapprove of the efficacy of one medicine, which was a popular prescription drug choice a few days back. So, the pharmaceutical industry has to maintain a supply chain which is adaptable to the whims and woes of public demand.

Can technology help?

For complicated industries like the pharmaceuticals, it is always recommended to adopt fast-moving RX technology, which empowers multi-scanning and rapid-screen at double the speed in half the time. What Is a Supply Chain?

A supply chain is a network between a company and its suppliers to produce and distribute a specific product to the final buyer. This network includes different activities, people, entities, information, and resources. 12. 2.Research and Explain the Business Buying Process by giving a real example,ProblemrecognitionGeneral needdescriptionProductspecificationSuppliersearchProposalsolicitationSupplierselectionOrder-routinespecificationPerformancereviewBusiness Buying Process​


Answer:

Problem Recognition is the first step in the consumer decision process. As business owners and/or marketers, our ability to recognize problems and solve them for our consumers, determines our success. Problem recognition is the result of a discrepancy between ones desired state and an actual state of satisfaction.

General need description is the stage in the business buying process in which a buyer describes the general characteristics and quantity of a needed item.” For standard items, this process presents a few problems.

A Product Specification, commonly referred to as a product spec, is an important product document that outlines key requirements for building a new feature, functionality, or product. ... An effective product spec is concise, brief, and not overly technical.

Supplier search is a stage of the business buying process in which the buyer tries to find the best vendors. The buyer can compile a small list of qualified suppliers by reviewing trade directories, doing computer searches, or phoning other companies for recommendations.

Proposal Solicitation means the process of advertising/requesting and receiving vendors' proposals submitted in response to an RFP or Letter of Configuration.

Supplier selection is the process by which firms identify, evaluate, and contract with suppliers. The supplier selection process deploys an enormous amount of a firm's financial resources and plays crucial role for the success of any organization.

Order-routine specification is a stage of the business buying process in which the buyer writes the final order with the chosen supplier(s), listing the technical specifications, quantity needed, expected time of delivery, return policies, and warranties.

A performance appraisal, also referred to as a performance review, performance evaluation, development discussion, or employee appraisal is a method by which the job performance of an employee is documented and evaluated.

The stages of business buying includes recognizing the problem, developing product specs to solve the problem, searching for possible products, selecting a supplier and ordering the product, and finally evaluating the product and supplier performance.

19. According to the law of supply, what happens to the quantity that the suppliers create when the prices increase? According to the law of supply, when prices increase, the quantity that suppliers create_______


Answer: According to the law of supply, when prices increase, the quantity that suppliers create will increase.

Explanation: In microeconomics, the law of supply governs the relationship between the price of a good or service and the quantity offered by its suppliers. All other factors being equal, an increase in the price of a good or service will lead to an increase in the quantity of the good or service on the market. The increase in price incentivizes producers to increase the quantity of the supply. The producers are incentivized by the desire to earn greater profit. If the price increases and the quantity supplied increases, the producers stand to earn more should the quantity supplied be completely sold off.
