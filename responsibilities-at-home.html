Answer:

Getting children to start learning household chores can start as early as pre-school age. When they're done playing with their toys, parents can teach in putting the toys away. When children are already teenagers, more households are expected from them to perform. The following are household chores that teenagers can do at home including: putting away their belongings,  doing the laundry , folding and putting away clean clothes,  vacuuming, sweeping, dusting , setting the table , clearing the table , washing and putting away the dishes , feeding, walking family pets, cleaning birdcages and litter boxes , mopping floors , scrubbing the sink, toilet, bathtub, shower stall , preparing their own lunches for schooling, playing chef and cooking dinner one night a week , doing hardwork , washing the family car.

Learning to help out at home teaches kids the importance of taking part and contributing in a team. It also gives them the sense of being valued and competent which boosts self-esteem.
