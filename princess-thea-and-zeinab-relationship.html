18. Thea is twice as old as vince, Ray is 2 years older than thea. Write an algebraic equation showing that twice the sum of thea and vince's ages will result to ray s age? ___.


Let's assume that Vince's age is x.

According to the problem, Thea's age is twice Vince's age, so Thea's age is 2x.

Ray is two years older than Thea, so Ray's age is (2x + 2).

Twice the sum of Thea and Vince's ages is 2(2x + x) = 6x.

So the algebraic equation showing that twice the sum of Thea and Vince's ages will result in Ray's age is:

6x = 2(2x + 2)

Simplifying the equation, we get:

6x = 4x + 4

2x = 4

x = 2

Therefore, Vince's age is 2, Thea's age is 2x = 4, and Ray's age is (2x + 2) = 6.

To check our solution, twice the sum of Thea and Vince's ages is 2(2 + 4) = 12, which is indeed equal to Ray's age of 6.
