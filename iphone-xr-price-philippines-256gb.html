[tex]6 + 2x = 62990\\6-6+2x= 62990 - 6\\2x = 62984\\\frac{2x}{2} = \frac{62984}{2} \\x = 31492[/tex]

Compute for the MARK UP % (nearest hundredths). MIL%​


Answer:

Markup is the difference between the cost price of a product and its selling price. It is usually expressed in monetary terms. For example, if a product costs $10 to make and is sold for $15, the markup on the product is $5.

Markup percentage, on the other hand, is a measure of the profit made on a product, expressed as a percentage of the cost price. To calculate the markup percentage, divide the markup by the cost price and multiply by 100. In the example above, the markup percentage would be (5 / 10) x 100 = 50%.

It's important to note that markup and markup percentage are not the same thing. Markup is a specific amount of money, while markup percentage is a ratio that shows the profit as a percentage of the cost price. I hope this helps! Let me know if you have any other questions.
