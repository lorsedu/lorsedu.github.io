2. what is the tone and mood ofthe story "The husband of mrs cruz"


TONE AND MOOD STORY OF THE HUSBAND OF MRS. CRUZ

The tone of F. Scott Fitzgerald's novella "The Husband of Mrs. Cruz" Sionil Jose is gloomy, depressed, and reflective. The tone of the narrative, which centers on the themes of love, grief, and loneliness, conveys Mrs. Cruz, the protagonist's intense emotions and internal struggle.

The narrative has a depressing and hopeless tone, as well as one of resignation and acceptance. The narrative depicts the intensity of Mrs. Cruz's grief and sense of solitude as a mother who has lost everything she has ever held dear, including her husband and son.

The narrative is also infused with a feeling of redemption and optimism. Mrs. Cruz takes comfort in the presence of her despite her losses.

The overwhelming sense of grief and despair that surrounds this story hit me as I read it. Mrs. Cruz is clearly grieving, and the author expertly captures the intensity of her suffering and sense of loneliness.

However, as the narrative progressed, I also started to experience a feeling of hope and redemption, as Mrs. Cruz seeks solace in the spirit of her late husband.

Overall, "The Husband of Mrs. Cruz" is a lovely and thought-provoking tale that serves as a reminder of the persistence of the human spirit and the universal sense of loss. It serves as a heartbreaking reminder that, despite sorrow, there is always the chance for hope and restoration.
