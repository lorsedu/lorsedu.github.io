The general formula for profit is

Profit = Revenue – Expenses

ۦۦۦۦۦۦۦۦۦۦ

Revenue in our cases is:

[tex]18000 × 6 + 12000 × 6 = 180000[/tex]

Expenses is the cost of bought smartphones with discount accounted for:

Profit = 180000 – 190000 = –10000

Negative profit is called loss.

8. A smartphone is purchased with a down payment of ₱1,000 and the balance at ₱1,075.83 a month for 1 year. What is its cash price if the interest rate is 6% converted monthly?


Answer:

₱13,499.995 or ₱13,500

Step-by-step explanation:

Cash Price = Down Payment + Present Value

Present Value = [tex]R\frac{1-(1+i)^{-n} }{i}[/tex]

[tex]i = \frac{r}{m}[/tex]

Down Payment = 1,000

R = 1,075.83

r = .06

n = 12

[tex]i = \frac{.06}{12}[/tex]

[tex]i = .005[/tex]

Present Value = [tex]1,075.83\frac{1-(1+.005)^{-12} }{.005}[/tex]

Present Value = 12,499.995 or 12,500

Cash Price = 1,000 + 12,499.995

                  = 13,499.995 or 13,500

Problem:Compute the monthly installment of the new smartphone cash price of ₽20,000 for 6 months with an interest rate of 6% compounded monthly.

Given:

PV = 20,000

time = 6/12 = 0.5 ; because it is installment for 6 months and there are 12 months in a year

m = 12 (because it is compounded monthly)

r = 6% = 6/100 = 0.06

i = r/m = 0.06/12 = 0.005

n = mt = 12(0.5) = 6

[tex]\[R = \frac{{PV}}{{\frac{{1 - {{(1 + i)}^{ - n}}}}{i}}}\][/tex]

[tex]\[R = \frac{{20000}}{{\frac{{1 - {{(1 + 0.005)}^{ - 6}}}}{{0.005}}}}\][/tex]

[tex]\[R = \frac{{20000}}{{\frac{{1 - {{(1.005)}^{ - 6}}}}{{0.005}}}}\][/tex]
