Answer:

Anaphora: "dogs" is repeated several times in the paragraph to emphasize the topic and main subject of the discussion.

Metaphor: "dogs are man's best friend" is a common metaphor used to describe the close bond between humans and dogs.

Personification: "dogs might not talk but they can sense everything and never judge" gives human characteristics, such as sensing and judging, to dogs.

Antithesis: "filled with problems and worried about tomorrow" is contrasted with "dogs might not talk but they can sense everything and never judge" to emphasize the difference between human and dog behavior.

Hyperbole: "dogs are unselfish and loves to accompany you in every situation" is an exaggerated statement that emphasizes the loyalty and companionship of dogs.

Symbolism: Dogs represent loyalty, care, love, and companionship in the paragraph, which are symbolic of the relationship between humans and dogs.
