9. Why do you think micommunication, war and diparity between and among nation/countrie i till oberved by technology?.


Answer:

It is not necessarily accurate to say that technology is the cause of conflict, war, and disparity between and among nations and countries. These issues have complex and multifaceted causes that involve a wide range of social, political, economic, and cultural factors.

Technology can play a role in exacerbating or mitigating these issues, depending on how it is used. For example, technology can be used to facilitate communication and understanding between people of different nations and cultures, which can help to reduce misunderstandings and promote cooperation. At the same time, technology can also be used to spread misinformation and propaganda, which can fuel conflict and contribute to misunderstandings between different groups.

Furthermore, the unequal distribution of technology and access to its benefits can contribute to disparities between and among nations and countries. Some countries and regions may have more advanced technologies and infrastructure, while others may be lagging behind, which can create imbalances in economic and political power.

Overall, the relationship between technology and conflict, war, and disparity is complex and multifaceted, and cannot be reduced to a simple cause and effect relationship.

Explanation:

Technology has greatly impacted communication, war, and disparity between and among nations and countries. Communication has been revolutionized with the introduction of new technologies such as social media, video conferencing, and other web-based technologies; it is now easier than ever to communicate and share information. This has enabled people from different countries to connect in ways never before possible.

War has also been greatly affected by technology. The invention of nuclear weapons has drastically changed the way we think about war, with its potentially devastating effects being felt across the world. Technology has also made war more efficient, allowing for greater precision and control over military operations.

Finally, technology has had an impact on the disparity between and among nations and countries. It has allowed less developed countries to access resources and knowledge that they may not have been able to otherwise. By connecting people from different countries and cultures, technology has helped bridge the gap between those living in different parts of the world.
