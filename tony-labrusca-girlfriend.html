15. Rogers ordered several meters of silk cloth to Tony for his project in school. Unfortunately, Tony has only 10 meters of cloth in stock. Tony bought the remaining lengths of the cloth to Stephen, a wholesaler, for P300. Tony then sold the cloth from Stephen along with the original stock that he has for a total of P750. If Tony's price per meter of cloth is at least P10 more than Stephen's price, what possible lengths of cloth did Tony buy from Stephen?​


Answer:

Let X be the number of meters of cloth that Tony bought from Stephen.

Tony paid P300 for X meters of cloth, so the price per meter of cloth that Tony paid is 300/X.

Tony sold the cloth that he bought from Stephen along with the original 10 meters of cloth that he had for a total of P750.

The total price for the original 10 meters of cloth that Tony had is 750 - 300 = P450.

The price per meter of cloth that Tony sold the cloth that he bought from Stephen for is 450/X.

The price per meter of cloth that Tony sold the cloth that he bought from Stephen for is at least P10 more than the price per meter of cloth that Tony paid, so 450/X >= 300/X + 10.

This simplifies to 150/X >= 10.

Dividing both sides by 15, we get 10/X >= 1.

Since X is the number of meters of cloth that Tony bought from Stephen, X must be at least 10 meters. Therefore, the possible lengths of cloth that Tony bought from Stephen are 10 meters or more.
