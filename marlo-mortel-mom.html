1. Marlo pays $750 rent a month. Bea's rent is 12% higher. What is the ratio of Marlo's rent to Bea's rent?


Answer:

25 : 28

Step-by-step explanation:

First we need to get the rent of Bea :

let x be the rent of Bea :

x = 750 + 750(12%)

x = 750 + 90

x = 840

Bea's rent is $840.

Now we'll just use ratio and proportion  to get ratio of Marlo's rent to Bea's rent :

Marlo's rent : Bea's rent

750 : 840

25 : 28

Thus the ratio of Marlo's rent to Bea's rent is 25 : 28.
