5. Write reflection inyour answer sheet (500 words) The World Health Organization (WHO) declared last March 15, 2020 that Corona Virus Disease (Covid-19) was considered as pandemic world wide. The international and domestic markets particularly our country was affected for more than a year resulted to economic recession since the unemployment rate of the country rose to 17.7% and Gross Domestic Product (GDP) registered -9.5% for 2020 according to Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA, 2021). As a senior high school student, how would you apply the concepts of monetary and fiscal policies to address the economics problem of the Philippines today.​


The Covid-19 pandemic has caused significant economic challenges not only in the Philippines but also across the world. As a senior high school student, it is essential to understand the concepts of monetary and fiscal policies and how they can help address the economic problems faced by the country today.

Monetary policy refers to the actions taken by the central bank of a country to control the money supply and achieve certain economic goals. One way to address the economic problems in the Philippines today is by implementing expansionary monetary policy. This involves increasing the money supply and reducing interest rates to encourage borrowing and spending. By lowering interest rates, businesses and individuals are more likely to invest and spend, which can stimulate economic growth. The central bank can also increase the money supply by purchasing government bonds or other financial assets, which can inject more money into the economy. However, there is a risk of inflation with this policy, which can reduce the value of the currency and increase the cost of living.

On the other hand, fiscal policy refers to the government's use of spending and taxation to influence the economy. One way to address the economic problems in the Philippines is through expansionary fiscal policy. This involves increasing government spending and reducing taxes to stimulate economic activity. By increasing government spending, the government can invest in infrastructure projects, healthcare, and education, which can create job opportunities and increase demand for goods and services. The reduction of taxes can also increase disposable income, which can encourage spending and stimulate economic growth. However, there is a risk of budget deficits with this policy, which can lead to an increase in government debt.

In addressing the economic problems of the Philippines today, it is important to strike a balance between monetary and fiscal policies. A combination of both expansionary monetary and fiscal policies may be necessary to stimulate the economy and create job opportunities. For instance, the central bank can implement expansionary monetary policies to reduce interest rates and increase the money supply, while the government can implement expansionary fiscal policies by increasing government spending on infrastructure, healthcare, and education.

However, it is important to note that implementing expansionary monetary and fiscal policies may have limitations. For instance, during the pandemic, businesses may be hesitant to invest or borrow due to uncertainty in the economy, and individuals may not be spending as much as they used to. Thus, it is important to also address the root cause of the economic problems, such as containing the spread of the virus and ensuring the safety of the population.

Overall, by implementing a combination of both short-term and long-term strategies, the country can work towards a sustainable and resilient economy despite the challenges posed by the pandemic. As a senior high school student, it is crucial to stay informed and engaged in understanding the economic issues and contribute to finding solutions to help the country's economic recovery.

Common Diseases During the Rainy Season

Rainy Season, or the wet season, is one among the four seasons when the region is average rainfall is achieved. It is generally caused due to changes in the flow of winds and other geological factors. Generally, the rainy season brings much-needed relief from the extreme heat of the preceding summer season, replenishing the rivers and lakes. Plants that were previously wilted spring to life in the rainy season.

Animals are also active during the rainy season. This is the season where farmers look forward to, as rain becomes the saving grace for their crops. However, the season also has the potential to bring about large scale destruction if preventive measures are not taken. Floods, hurricanes, storms and other water-related natural calamities usually occur during the rainy season.

Historically, people have always associated the rainy season with the growth of vegetation. However, from an agricultural perspective, food crops do not reach their full maturity and it can cause food shortages in some parts of the world.

During this season, an increase in the incidence of malaria and other water-borne diseases are also observed. The sudden onset of monsoon can also make people prone to other diseases such as jaundice, typhoid, and cholera.

9. 2. Enrolment Rate in the Philippines for School Year 2020-2021​


Answer:

90.14 percent

Explanation:

DepEd national enrollment data as of October 26, 2020 showed that there are 25.03 million students enrolled for School Year (SY) 2020-2021 which formally opened on Oct. 5. This enrollment figure, DepEd said, covers both public and private schools and comprises 90.14 percent of SY 2019-2020 turnout at 27.7 million.

In the public school system, DepEd said that the enrollment has reached 22.75 million – exceeding SY 2019-2020 enrollment at 22.5 million. Enrollment in private schools, DepEd explained, is also picking up with 2.22 million enrollees or 51.66% of last school year’s enrollment turnout.

Currently, DepEd data showed that there are 1.8 million enrollees in Kindergarten; 11.9 million in Elementary; 7.9 million in Junior High School (JHS); and 2.8 million in Senior High School (SHS). A total of 73, 195 non-graded learners with disabilities are also enrolled along with 405, 402 students under the ALS program.

DepEd latest data showed that highest numbers of enrollees for this SY are from Region IV-A (Calabarzon) with 3.4 million followed by National Capital Region (NCR) with 2.6 million and Region III (Central Luzon) with 2.5 million.
