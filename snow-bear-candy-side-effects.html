10. 4. Phil bought gummy bear candies of different colors - 6 red, 3 green, and 7 yellow. What is the probability that he will pick a red candy?​


There are a total of 6 + 3 + 7 = 16 gummy bear candies. The probability of picking a red candy can be calculated as:

probability of red candy = (number of red candies) / (total number of candies)

probability of red candy = 6 / 16

probability of red candy = 0.375 or 37.5%

Therefore, the probability that Phil will pick a red candy is 0.375 or 37.5%.

29. At a candy store, Erika bought 3 kilograms of cinnamon red hots and 1 kilogram of gummy bears for $21.00. Meanwhile, Irene bought 3 kilograms of cinnamon red hots and 3 kilograms of gummy bears for $39.00. What is the cost of one kilogram of each type of candy?


Answer:

Cinnamon red hots - $4

Gummy bears - $9

Step-by-step explanation:

a and b represents the price of cinnamon red hots and gummy bears respectively.

3 kilograms of cinnamon red hots and 1 kilogram of gummy bears for $21.00:

3a+b=21

3 kilograms of cinnamon red hots and 3 kilograms of gummy bears for $39.00:

3a+3b=39

Solve by elimination:

(3a+3b)-(3a+b)=39-21

2b=18

b=9

Substitute the value of b to one of the equations:

3a+9=21

3a=12

a=4
