8. Amanda was task to buy vegetables in a nearby flea market. To do this she had to take walk about 55m east to reach her friends house. Both of them will walk another 220m to reach the flea market. Assuming that they got there in 5minutes, how fast did Amanda and her friend were walking? What is Amanda's total distance? What is the displacement? Illustrate Amanda's path of travel. What is Amanda's speed and velocity?​


Explanation:

To find the speed at which Amanda and her friend were walking, we need to use the formula:

speed = distance / time

We know that Amanda walked 55m to reach her friend's house, and then both of them walked another 220m to reach the flea market. So Amanda's total distance is:

55m + 220m = 275m

We also know that they got to the flea market in 5 minutes. To find their speed, we need to convert the time to seconds:

5 minutes x 60 seconds/minute = 300 seconds

So their speed is:

speed = distance / time = 275m / 300s = 0.92 m/s

To find the displacement, we need to find the straight-line distance from Amanda's starting point to the flea market. We can use the Pythagorean theorem to do this:

displacement = sqrt(55^2 + 220^2) = 227.57m

To illustrate Amanda's path of travel, we can draw a straight line from her starting point to the flea market.

Amanda's speed is 0.92 m/s. Her velocity is the speed in a particular direction. Since she walked east to reach her friend's house and then continued east to reach the flea market, her velocity is also eastward, and the magnitude is 0.92 m/s.
