Answer:

Bail can be a verb or a noun. When you bail (verb) someone out of jail, you post money, also known as bail (noun), to assure the authorities that person won't try to run away before going to trial.

When you bail (verb) someone out of jail, you post money, also known as bail (noun), to assure the authorities that person won't try to run away before going to trial.As a noun, bail means bond money. This comes from the Middle English word bayle, for "captivity." As a verb, the idea of "bailing out" comes from the later word baile, which means “dipping out,” a meaning you can use when you are trying to empty something flooded. 12. introduction paragraph of low bail for the poor​


Answer:

Low bail for the poor is a contentious issue that has gained significant attention in recent years. The bail system is designed to ensure that individuals who are arrested and charged with a crime appear in court and do not flee. However, this system often disproportionately impacts those who are socio-economically disadvantaged. Many people who cannot afford to pay bail end up spending time in jail, even if they have not been convicted of a crime. This can lead to lost income, lost jobs, and significant hardship for individuals and their families. As a result, there has been a growing movement to reform the bail system and make it more equitable for all individuals, regardless of their financial situation. This essay will explore the issue of low bail for the poor, the arguments for and against it, and the potential implications of this reform on the criminal justice system.
