12. Pretend that you are in camping or any recreational activities and unexpectedly there is an accident. So now you will be the first aider, what will you do?


Answer:

1. Assess the situation: Make sure the area is safe and secure. Quickly scan the scene and determine how many people are injured and how serious the injuries are.

2. Activate emergency services: If the injury is serious or life-threatening, call 911 or your local emergency service immediately.

3. Administer first aid: Evaluate the victims and start with basic first aid like calling for help, providing direct pressure for severe bleeding and checking for any signs of life.

4. Care for the Andjured: Look for signs of shock and provide comfort to the injured person by keeping them warm and refrain from moving them if possible.

5. Protect from further injury: Make sure to create a barrier with blankets or other materials to shield the victim from any further harm.

6. Monitor the person's vitals: Stay nearby while keeping a close eye on the person's heart rate, breathing rate, and overall condition.

7. Notify next-of-kin: If you know who the injured person's family and friends are, notify them of the situation and keep them updated until the medical team arrives.

19. differentiate boot sequence vs. boot menu?​


Answer:

boot sequence defines which devices a computer should check for the operating system's boot files. It also specifies the order devices are checked.

The Boot Menu is a menu accessible when a computer is first starting up. It can contain many different device options to boot to, including CD, DVD, flash drive, or hard drives, and a LAN (network). The Boot Menu allows a user to load up other operating systems or applications, even if the computer itself already has an operating system on it.
