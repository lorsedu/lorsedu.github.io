15. Mother bought 3 ⅓ dozen apples. She served ¼ of the apple to Aljur friends. How many apples remained? with solution please


Answer:

There are different ways to approach this problem, but one possible method is to use fractions and multiplication to find the number of apples served and the number of apples remaining.

First, let's convert 3 ⅓ dozen apples to the total number of apples. One dozen is equal to 12, so:

3 ⅓ dozen = 3 x 12 + ⅓ x 12 = 36 + 4 = 40

So, Mother bought 40 apples.

Next, let's find the number of apples served to Aljur's friends. We are told that 1/4 of the apples were served, so:

Number of apples served = (1/4) x 40 = 10

Finally, to find the number of apples remaining, we can subtract the number of apples served from the total number of apples:

Number of apples remaining = 40 - 10 = 30

So, there were 30 apples remaining.

Therefore, the answer is: There were 30 apples that remained.
