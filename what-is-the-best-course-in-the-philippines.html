2. The Graduate Record Exam (GRE) is a standardized test required to be admitted to many graduate schools in the United States. A high score in the GRE makes admission more likely. According to the Educational Testing Service, the mean score for takers of GRE who do not have training courses is 555 with a standard deviation of 139. Brain Philippines (BP) offers expensive GRE training courses, claiming their graduates score better than those who have not taken any training courses. To test the company's claim, a statistician randomly selected 40 graduates of BP and asked their GRE scores and the random sample of 40 graduates he obtains recorded a mean score of 560 in GRE. Test at 5% level of significance. 1. What is the appropriate Alternative hypothesis for this problem? A. u = 555 B. u < 555 C. u > 555 D. u /= 555 2. What test statistic will be used for the test? A one-tailed z-test B. one-tailed t-test C. two-tailed z-test D two-tailed t test 3. What is the computed test statistic? A. 0 2275 B. -0.2275 C. 2.9928 D. -2.9928 4. What is the critical value/s at 5% level of significance? (if two answers are possible, ENTER BOTH LETTERS ONLY eg: XY, YZ, ZY, YX) A 1.96 B 1.645 C. -1.96 D. -1 645 5. Based on the computation and decision rule, what is the best decision? A. Accept Ho B. Reject Ho C. Fail to reject Ho D. Fail to Reject Ha


Answer:

1.) D. u /= 555

This means that the graduates who have taken the BP GRE training course have a different mean GRE score from those who have not taken any training courses.

2.) B. one-tailed t-test

Since the population standard deviation is unknown and the sample size is less than 30, we use a t-test.

3.) 0.53

To compute the t-test statistic, we use the formula t = (x̄ - μ) / (s / √n), where x̄ is the sample mean, μ is the hypothesized population mean, s is the sample standard deviation, and n is the sample size.

t = (560 - 555) / (139 / √40) = 0.53

None of the choices

4.) B. 1.645

At 5% level of significance with a one-tailed test, the critical value for a t-test with 39 degrees of freedom is 1.645.

5.) C. Fail to reject Ho

Since the computed t-test statistic of 0.53 is less than the critical value of 1.645, we fail to reject the null hypothesis. There is insufficient evidence to conclude that the mean GRE score of BP graduates who have taken the training course is different from the mean GRE score of those who have not taken any training courses at a 5% level of significance.
