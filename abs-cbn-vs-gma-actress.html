6. 2. In a certain city, 60% of the residents watching either ABS-CBN or GMA on the television. Thirty percent of them watching ABS-CBN. Forty four percent are watching GMA. What percentage of the residents are watching both channels?​


Answer:

14%

Step-by-step explanation:


To solve this problem, we can use the formula for calculating the probability of the intersection of two events:

P(A ∩ B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A ∪ B)

where P(A) represents the probability of event A, P(B) represents the probability of event B, and P(A ∪ B) represents the probability of the union of events A and B.

Let A represent the event of watching ABS-CBN and B represent the event of watching GMA. Then, we are given:

P(A ∪ B) = 0.60 (60% of residents are watching either ABS-CBN or GMA)

P(A) = 0.30 (30% of residents are watching ABS-CBN)

P(B) = 0.44 (44% of residents are watching GMA)

We can plug these values into the formula and solve for P(A ∩ B):

P(A ∩ B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A ∪ B)

P(A ∩ B) = 0.30 + 0.44 - 0.60

P(A ∩ B) = 0.14

Therefore, the percentage of residents watching both ABS-CBN and GMA is 14%.
