9. 9) A lottery game is set up such that each player chooses 7 different numbers from 1 to 58. With one ticket, what is the probability of winning the prize? 10) Trish forgot the last two digits of her password on a website. If she chooses a two- digit number at random, what is the probability that her choice is correct?​


Answer:

9. To calculate the probability of winning the lottery prize, we need to determine the total number of possible outcomes and the number of favorable outcomes.

Total number of possible outcomes: There are 58 numbers to choose from, and the player must choose 7 different numbers. This can be calculated using the combination formula:

C(58,7) = 58! / (7! * (58-7)!) = 85,900,584

Favorable outcomes: There is only one winning combination, so there is only one favorable outcome.

Therefore, the probability of winning the lottery prize with one ticket is:

P(winning) = favorable outcomes / total outcomes = 1 / 85,900,584

So the probability of winning the lottery prize with one ticket is approximately 0.00000116, or 1 in 85,900,584.

10. Trish forgot the last two digits of her password on a website, so there are 100 possible choices for the two-digit number. Since she doesn't remember the last two digits, we can assume that all the choices are equally likely.

The probability of choosing the correct two-digit number is:

P(correct) = 1 / 100

So the probability that Trish's choice is correct is 0.01, or 1%.

17. Suppose a cereal company puts a prize in each box. There are four possible prizes, and  you want to get two identical prizes. How many boxes would you need to purchase to  guarantee you get two identical prizes? Say the prizes are A, B, C, and D. Pls answer report if nonsense​


HOW MANY BOXES IS THE GUARANTEE?

Problem:

Suppose a cereal company puts a prize in each box. There are four possible prizes, and  you want to get two identical prizes. How many boxes would you need to purchase to guarantee you get two identical prizes? Say the prizes are A, B, C, and D.

Answer with Step-by-step explanation:

At first look, you might say that this word problem will require long calculations. Actually, this only requires common sense and critical thinking skills. So here is the scenario. The company's prizes in each box are maybe A, B, C and D, and let us say that in the first 4 boxes that you bought and opened, you have all 4 unique prizes. On the fifth box, you may either open prize A, B, C or D by which you will already have two identical prizes.

Therefore, you are guaranteed to have two identical prizes of that certain cereal company when you purchased 5 cereal boxes.
