PROBLEM SOLVING

[tex]\normalsize{\blue{\overline{\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad}}}[/tex]

[tex]\rm{\underline{PROBLEM:}}[/tex]

A bag costs Php 410.65. A necklace costs Php 356.95 more than the bag. Ann wants to buy 3 such necklaces. How much money does she have to pay for the 3 necklaces?

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\rm{\underline{QUESTIONS:}}[/tex]

(A.) What is asked?

[tex]\boxed{\begin{gathered} \begin{array}{ccc} \rm{\bullet \: How \: much \: money \: does \: she \: have \: to \: pay \: for \: the \: 3 \: necklaces?} \\ \end{array} \end{gathered}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

(B.) What are given?

[tex]\begin{gathered}\boxed{\begin{array}{ccc} \rm{ \bullet \: A \: bag \: costs \: Php \: 410.65 \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ }\\ \\ \rm{ \bullet \: A \: necklace \: costs \: Php \: 356.95 \: more \: than \: the \: bag} \\ \end{array}}\end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

(C.) Hidden Question:

[tex]\boxed{\begin{gathered} \begin{array}{ccc} \rm{\bullet \: How \: much \: does \: 1 \: necklace \: cost?} \\ \end{array} \end{gathered}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

(D.) Operation(s):

[tex]\begin{gathered}\boxed{\begin{array}{ccc} \rm{ \bullet \: Addition (+) \ \ \ \ \ \ \ } \\ \\ \rm{ \bullet \: Multiplication ( \times ) } \\ \end{array}}\end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

(E.) Number Sentence:

[tex]\begin{gathered}\boxed{\begin{array}{ccc} \underline{\rm{To \: find \: the \: price \: of \: 1 \: necklace :}}\\ \\ \rm{ \bullet \: 410.65 + 356.95 = ?} \\ \end{array}}\end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{gathered}\boxed{\begin{array}{ccc} \underline{\rm{To \: find \: the \: total\: price \: of \: 3 \: necklaces :}}\\ \\ \rm{ \bullet \: (410.65 + 356.95) \times 3 = ?} \\ \end{array}}\end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

(F.) What is the answer?​

To find the total money she has to pay, first we should find how much the necklace costs, then multiply the sum three times. The product will be the final answer.

First find the price of one necklace by adding the cost of the bag (Php 410.65) and the cost of one necklace more than the bag (Php 356.95)

[tex]\begin{gathered}\boxed{\begin{array}{ccc} \rm{\: \: \blue{ \ \ \ \ \: \ \ 1 \ \ 1 }} \\ \rm{\: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: 410 \: . \: 65} \\ \rm{+ \underline{\: \: \: \: \: \: 356 \: . \: 95}} \\ \rm{\: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \green{767 \: . \: 60}} \\ \end{array}}\end{gathered}[/tex]

So, one necklace costs Php 767.60

[tex]\\[/tex]Now multiply 767.60 three times since we're looking for the price of three necklaces.

[tex]\begin{gathered}\boxed{\begin{array}{ccc} \rm{\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ 76760} \\ \times \rm{\ \ \underline{\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \: 3}} \\ \rm{\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ 230280} \\ \rm{ \ \ \ \ \ \overline{ \ \ \ \: 230280}}\end{array}}\end{gathered}[/tex]

Since there are 2 decimal places, rewrite 230280 as 2302.80.

[tex]\\[/tex]

Hence, the total money she has to pay for 3 necklaces is Php 2302.80.
