24. What are the terms used by PAGASA to describe the weather condition?


PAGASA (Philippine Atmospheric Geophysical and Astronomical Services Administration) is a Philippine institution that is in-charge of delivering the weather conditions, advisories, and forecasts in all major places and affected areas in the Philippines. They also provide information on climatological, astronomical, meteorological conditions and events that may or might happen or just pure updates. Reporting all of these serves a guide, a reminder, and alert about the day’s or week’s weather and for the protection and safety of life and property of the country. Their deliverance are not only available on televisions and radios, but also on social media sites and on their own website.

PAGASA uses technical terms in describing and delivering the reports and updates about the weather. It is divided into four (4) categories: A. Cloud Description, B. Precipitation Description, C. Wind Description, and D. Sea Condition as affected by Wind Force. Each category contains specific terms to be used.

A. Cloud Description

*Clear or Sunny Skies

*Partly Cloudy

*Partly Cloudy to At Times Cloudy

*Mostly or Mainly Cloudy

*Cloudy

*Overcast

B. Precipitation Description

I. Rains

  *Very Light Rains

  *Light Rains

  *Moderate Rains

  *Heavy Rains

  *Monsoon Rains

  *Occasional Rains

  *Widespread Rains

  *Frequent Rains

  *Intermittent Rains

 

II. Rainshowers

   *Scattered Rainshowers

   *Isolated Rainshowers

   *Occasional Rainshowers

   *Squally Rainshowers

C. Wind Description

*Light Winds

*Moderate Winds

*Moderate to Occasionally Strong

*Fresh Winds

*Strong Winds

*Gale (Near Gale, Gale, Strong Gale)

*Stormy (Storm, Violent Storm, Typhoon)

*Gust

D. Sea Condition as affected by Wind Force

*Calm

*Smooth

*Slight

*Moderate

*Rough

*Very Rough

*High

*Very High

*Phenomenal
