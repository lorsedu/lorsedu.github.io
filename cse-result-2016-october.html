14. roles of school and parents/guardians in cse (comprehensive sexuality education)program.​


Answer:

The Comprehensive Sexuality Education (CSE) program aims to provide young people with accurate, age-appropriate, and culturally sensitive information about sexuality and relationships. The program is a joint responsibility of schools and parents/guardians, and both have important roles to play.

The roles of schools in CSE include:

1. Providing a safe and inclusive learning environment where students can ask questions, express their views, and receive accurate and non-judgmental information about sexuality and relationships.

2. Developing and implementing a comprehensive CSE curriculum that is age-appropriate, culturally sensitive, and evidence-based.

3. Ensuring that teachers and other school staff receive adequate training on CSE, including how to teach the subject matter effectively, address sensitive topics, and respond to students' questions and concerns.

4. Encouraging the involvement of parents/guardians in the CSE program, such as by inviting them to parent-teacher meetings, providing them with information about the curriculum, and seeking their feedback and input.

The roles of parents/guardians in CSE include:

1. Being involved in their children's education and supporting their learning about sexuality and relationships.

2. Providing accurate and age-appropriate information about sexuality and relationships based on their cultural and religious values and beliefs.

3. Encouraging open communication with their children about sexuality and relationships, including answering their questions, listening to their concerns, and respecting their opinions.

4. Monitoring their children's access to media and technology and helping them navigate potential risks and challenges related to sexuality and relationships.

Overall, the collaboration between schools and parents/guardians is crucial to the success of CSE programs. By working together, they can ensure that young people receive accurate, comprehensive, and inclusive information about sexuality and relationships, and are equipped with the knowledge and skills they need to make informed decisions and lead healthy and fulfilling lives.

23. A client arrives at the prenatal clinic for the first prenatal assessment. The client tells the nurse that the first day of her last period was October 20th 2016. Using the naegele's rule, the nurse determines the estimated date of birth is which


NAEGELE'S RULE

Naegele's rule is one method used to determine the expected delivery date (EDD) based on the mother's first day of her last menstrual period (LMP).

It requires a series of steps how to determine the expected delivery date. I'll guide you all the way, and let's figure it out together!

Step 1: Determine the mother's last menstrual period. In the case scenario, the client's first day of her last period was October 20, 2016.

Step 2: Diminish three calendar months from the first day of her last menstrual period.

October 20, 2016 - 3 months = July 20, 2016

Step 3: Add seven days from the date you've calculated in step 2.

July 20, 2016 + 7 days = July 27, 2016

Step 4: Add one year from the date you've calculated in step 3.

July 27, 2016 + 1 year = July 27, 2017

ANSWER

Therefore, the estimated date of birth of the mother will be on July 27, 2017.

Important considerations that you should remember:

Naegele's rule is based on the calculation of a woman with a regular menstrual cycle of 28 days. Most women have a shorter or longer period of menstrual cycle, so it varies for every woman, especially those with an irregular menstrual cycle. Naegele's rule is based on the standard gestation period of 240 days (40 weeks). First-time mothers usually have a more extended gestational period. The differences in gestational period will affect the EDD calculations. Naegele's rule is not 100% accurate. It only estimates the expected date of the delivery. The most precise way of determining the due date of pregnancy is by having a regular ultrasound with your obstetrician. Ultrasound will let you know how far along you will be since it is based on the specific measurements of the growing fetus.

28. what is comprehensive sexuality education (cse)​


Comprehensive sexuality education (CSE) is a holistic approach to teaching about sex and relationships.
