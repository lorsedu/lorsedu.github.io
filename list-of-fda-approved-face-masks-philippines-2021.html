Answer:

One example of a toxic/hazardous product that has been found to have bad side effects and is not approved by the FDA (Food and Drug Administration) is Triclosan. Triclosan is an antimicrobial chemical that is commonly found in many household and personal care products such as soap, toothpaste, and deodorant. The FDA has banned the use of triclosan in soap and body wash products, and it can no longer be marketed as an ingredient in these products. However, it can still be found in other products such as toothpaste, deodorant, and other cosmetics.

The reason for the ban is that studies have found that triclosan can have negative effects on human health and the environment. It is considered an endocrine disruptor, which means it can interfere with the normal function of hormones in the body. It has also been found to promote antibiotic resistance, which can make it more difficult to treat bacterial infections. Additionally, it has been found to be toxic to aquatic organisms, and it can accumulate in the environment.

It's important to be aware of the products you're using and to check the ingredients list. If a product contains Triclosan, it's best to avoid it. Also, you should always check the safety and efficacy of a product by checking the FDA website and other reliable sources.
