8. what did you learn about financial statements? write a 150 word essay.deadline: today 5pm​


Financial statements are important documents that provide a snapshot of a company's financial health. There are three main types of financial statements: the balance sheet, income statement, and cash flow statement.

The balance sheet shows a company's assets, liabilities, and equity at a specific point in time. The income statement shows the company's revenue, expenses, and net income over a period of time. The cash flow statement shows the inflows and outflows of cash for a specific period.

Analyzing financial statements can help investors and stakeholders make informed decisions about a company's financial health and potential for growth. It can also help identify areas where a company may need to make changes to improve its financial performance.

Overall, financial statements are crucial for understanding a company's financial position and performance. They provide valuable insights into a company's operations and can help guide decision-making for investors, stakeholders, and management.

Here is a handy formula list for some commonly used financial ratios:

Current Ratio:

Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities

Quick Ratio:

Quick Ratio = (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities

Debt-to-Equity Ratio:

Debt-to-Equity Ratio = Total Debt / Shareholders' Equity

Debt-to-Asset Ratio:

Debt-to-Asset Ratio = Total Debt / Total Assets

Return on Equity (ROE):

ROE = Net Income / Shareholders' Equity

Return on Assets (ROA):

ROA = Net Income / Total Assets

Gross Profit Margin:

Gross Profit Margin = Gross Profit / Total Revenue

Net Profit Margin:

Net Profit Margin = Net Income / Total Revenue

Price-to-Earnings Ratio (P/E Ratio):

P/E Ratio = Market Price per Share / Earnings per Share

Dividend Yield:

Dividend Yield = Dividend per Share / Market Price per Share

Price-to-Sales Ratio (P/S Ratio):

P/S Ratio = Market Price per Share / Sales per Share

Earnings per Share (EPS):

EPS = Net Income / Number of Shares Outstanding

Price-to-Book Ratio (P/B Ratio):

P/B Ratio = Market Price per Share / Book Value per Share

18. what is ancient greek anatomy and mathematical precision?deadline: today 5pm​


Ancient Greek anatomy and mathematical precision were closely linked, as the Greeks believed that the human body was a product of divine design and that it could be understood through mathematical principles.

In terms of anatomy, ancient Greek physicians and philosophers made significant contributions to the study of human anatomy. The Greek physician Hippocrates, for example, is considered the father of modern medicine and is credited with developing the Hippocratic Corpus, a collection of medical texts that established the principles of medical ethics and the practice of clinical observation.

The Greek philosopher Plato also wrote extensively about the human body, including its connection to the soul and its role in human cognition. He believed that the body was a material manifestation of the soul and that the study of the body could reveal important insights into the nature of the human mind.

In terms of mathematical precision, the Greeks were known for their use of geometric principles to describe the human body. The physician Galen, for example, used geometry to measure the proportions of the human body and to describe the shapes of various organs. He believed that the study of geometry was essential to understanding the structure and function of the human body.

Overall, ancient Greek anatomy and mathematical precision were closely intertwined, as the Greeks believed that the study of the human body could reveal important insights into the nature of the universe and the divine principles that govern it.
