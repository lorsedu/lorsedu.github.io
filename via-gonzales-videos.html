What is Video Conferencing?

Video Conferencing is a communication program that allows people to meet each other in their own areas in one virtual room. Just like a normal meeting, everyone in the virtual room can speak with the mute and unmute button, share their presentation through a screen that everyone in the virtual room will see, and more features.

Advantages of using Video Conferencing

Convenient - Video Conferencing applications are all available in laptops, tablets, and smartphones.

No need to meet in one place - Especially with this COVID-19 pandemic and no gatherings even important ones are allowed.

Quiet - People in the virtual room can unmute their microphones when they want to speak and mute them if it is not needed.

Disadvantages of Using Video Conferencing

Internet Connection - Video Conferencing application needs internet connection to work so this will be a big disadvantage if your area has slow internet.

Technical Problems - If your device is laggy or having some problems, it will not allow video conferencing apps to work.

Expensive - The application itself might be free, but the devices and internet connection that is needed for the application to work costs a lot.

What is Audio Conferencing?

Audio Conferencing is a communication program that allows two or more people to call each other in their own areas. But unlike video conferencing, audio conferencing only allows people to call via audio, meaning they cannot see each other.

Advantages of Using Audio Conferencing

Convenient - Audio Conferencing applications are all available in laptops, tablets, and smartphones.

Good Audio Quality - Since audio conferencing focuses on audio, you can clearly hear what you're talking to is saying.

Easier access - Audio conferencing doesn't need that much long setup before officially starting unlike video conferencing where you have to setup everything.

Disadvantages of Using Audio Conferencing

Cannot see each other virtually - If you want to see someone even in virtual, then audio conferencing is not for you since it does not allow video calls and meetings

Technical Problems - If your device is laggy or having some problems, it will not allow audio conferencing apps to work.

Microphone Problems - If you or someone you're talking to experienced microphone problems, it will ruin your communication with each other since you or someone you're talking to cannot hear anything.
