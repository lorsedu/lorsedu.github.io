27. The available purchasing power in an economy depends on current income, prices, savings, debt, and credit availability. True or false.


True, The amount of money that customers have available to them to buy products and services is known as their "consumer purchasing power." It reflects how much inflation has an impact on consumers' ability to purchase goods and is tied to the cost of living index. A relative measure, purchasing power is the most pertinent when looking at trends across time. Numerous economic factors have an impact on these shifts in purchasing power.

• Real Income

Real income, or an individual's income that has been adjusted for inflation, determines their purchasing power.

• Prices

One of the key elements affecting a consumer's ability to make purchases is the price of the goods and services. As long as other things remain constant, purchasing power rises when prices down and declines when prices rise.

• Savings

Consumers can lessen the unpredictable nature of an unmanageable world by saving. Having money indicates that the customer can utilize it as needed, which gives them control over their spending that would not be possible without this resource.

• Debt

Debt actually reduces a person's real income. As a result, people with higher debt have less money to spend and have less purchasing power.

• Credit availability

Customers will look for financial aid from a bank or other organization if they are unable to afford the good or service they actually need. As a result, the availability of credit from financial institutions to individuals and businesses has an impact on overall spending power similarly to an increase in income.
