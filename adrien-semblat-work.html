12. Adrien (gets, get) excited whenever he's with his friends.


: Answer ꒱꒱ ̣ ̟

gets

Adrien gets excited whenever he's with his friends.

: Explanation ꒱꒱ ̣ ̟

Why is "gets" the correct answer?

In the sentence above, the subject (Adrien) is singular, this means that the subject is only one (1). The verb that we should use is "gets" because it is also singular.

Why should both the subject and verb be singular/plural?

Both the subject and verb in a sentence must be singular/plural (meaning that if the subject is singular its corresponding verb should also be singular and vise versa). This is called a subject-verb agreement.

But why is applying the subject-verb agreement in our sentences so important? The simple answer for this is because without it the reader/listeners may get confused and misunderstand what you say.

19. 5. Adrien's allowance for personal hygiene is P500.00. She bought 21/4 liters of alcoholat P65.00 per liter. How much money did she still have for face mask and otherthings?​


Answer:

P353.75

Step-by-step explanation:

Given

500 - Adrien's allowance for personal hygiene

2 1/4 - liters of alcohol she bought

65 - price of alcohol per liter

? - money she still have for facemask and other things

Solution

Get the total price of alcohol

- 2 1/4 × 65

> 2 × 65 = 130

- 1/4 x 65

Convert 1/4 to a decimal point

> 1/4 will be .25

> .25 x 65 = 16.25

Proceed to Addition

130 + 16.25 = 146.25

> 146.25 - Total cost of alcohol

Subtract the total cost of alcohol from Adrien's allowance

500 - 146.25

= 353.75 - money she still have for face mask and other things
