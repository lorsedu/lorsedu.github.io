16. 6) Pasta should be cook al dente or to the tooth ||abrenica pasta should be cook al dente or to the tooth.|| [ True or False ]Nonsense-ReportCorrect Answer-Brainliest​


Answer: False

Explanation:

Al dente (Italian for “to the tooth”) is where pasta tastes and feels the best. It’s chewy and firm, holding its whole shape in whatever sauce you put it in. And we always finish our pasta by tossing it in a pan of sauce. If you think about it though, that sauce in the pan cooks the pasta a second time. That’s why we never boil our pasta to al dente.

Molto al dente is what you want. That’s undercooked pasta, about three minutes from being that chewy al dente we love. If you pull a piece pasta from your pot, bite into it. There should be a chalky, gritty quality to the middle of the pasta, and you’ll be able to see it. If you look at the cross section of the noodle (where you bit), you should be able to see a thin white ring in the middle. That’s the stuff we like to see.

23. Mother bought 3 ⅓ dozen apples. She served ¼ of the apple to Aljur friends. How many apples remained? with solution please


Answer:

There are different ways to approach this problem, but one possible method is to use fractions and multiplication to find the number of apples served and the number of apples remaining.

First, let's convert 3 ⅓ dozen apples to the total number of apples. One dozen is equal to 12, so:

3 ⅓ dozen = 3 x 12 + ⅓ x 12 = 36 + 4 = 40

So, Mother bought 40 apples.

Next, let's find the number of apples served to Aljur's friends. We are told that 1/4 of the apples were served, so:

Number of apples served = (1/4) x 40 = 10

Finally, to find the number of apples remaining, we can subtract the number of apples served from the total number of apples:

Number of apples remaining = 40 - 10 = 30

So, there were 30 apples remaining.

Therefore, the answer is: There were 30 apples that remained.
