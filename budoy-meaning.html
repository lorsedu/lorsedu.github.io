6. budoy, a junior high school student lifts a 3 kg book from the floor into a cabinet 2.0m high. With reference to the floor how much potential energy does the book acquire?


Good Day

Problem:

budoy, a junior high school student lifts a 3 kg book from the floor into a cabinet 2.0m high. With reference to the floor how much potential energy does the book acquire?

Given:

m (mass) = 3 kg

g (gravity)  = 9.8 m/s²

h (height)  = 2.0 m

P.E. (potential energy)  = ? unknown

Solution:

The formula for P.E. (potential energy) , P.E = m × g × h

P.E.  = m × g × h

       = 3 kg × 9.8 m/s² × 2.0 m

       = 58.8 kg.m²/s²

      = 58.8 J

Answer:

Potential Energy = 58.8 J


7. How long does it take Budoy's car to change its velocity from 12 m/s to 25 m/s if it accelerates at 5 m/s^2


Answer:

It will take Budoy's car 2.6 seconds.

Explanation:

a = (Vf - Vi)/t

transpose formula

at = Vf - Vi

then divide both sides by a

t = (Vf - Vi)/a

Whereas:

a = acceleration

t = time

Vf = Final Velocity

Vi = Initial Velocity

Given:

a = 5m/s^2

Vf = 25 m/s

Vi = 12 m/s

Solution

(5m/s^2)t = 25 m/s - 12 m/s

(5m/s^2)t = 13 m/s

then Divide both sides by the acceleration thus:

t = 13m/s/5m/s^2

t = 2.6 sec.

13. How long does it take for Budoy's Car to change its velocity from 12m/s to 25 m/s if it accelerates at 5m/s?


Answer:

The time for Budoy's car to accelerate is 2.6 seconds

Explanation:

Here, we are to use a simple formula for average acceleration to solve what is being asked.

For the formula, we use:

a = Δv/ Δt

a = (Vf - Vi) / t

where

a     is the acceleration, unit is in m/s²

Vf   is the final velocity, unit is in m/s

Vi   is the initial velocity, unit is in m/s

t      is the time, unit here is in seconds

For the given information

Vi = 12 m/s

Vf = 25 m/s

a = 5 m/s²

t = ?

Solving the problem

Now, let us solve for the time using the formula and substituting the given information, we have:

a = (Vf - Vi) / t

5 m/s² = (25 m/s - 12 m/s) / t

Cross - multiply

t = (25 m/s - 12 m/s) / 5 m/s²

t = 2.6 seconds

Therefore, the time for Budoy's car to accelerate is 2.6 seconds.

Know that we know its definition, we know understand that your potential energy may differ on the point of reference you are using. Example, if you are on the rooftop of a 7-storey apartment, your potential energy will vary from each floor. Your potential energy from the rooftop in reference to the 5th floor will be different from your potential energy if your reference is the 2nd floor.
