Step-by-step explanation:

Using substitution, we do the following:

[tex]P +J = 27; 2P+ J =43\\J+P=27; J +2P=43\\J+P=27\\J+P+-P=27-P\\J+P-P=27-P\\J=27-P\\J+2P=43\\(27-P)+2P=43\\27+2P-P=43\\27+2P-1P=43\\27+1P=43\\27+P=43\\27+P-27=43-27\\P=16\\J=27-P\\J=27-16\\J=11[/tex].

Thus, Paul is 16 and Judy is 11.

With the information provided, we can make two algebraic equations:

M = 3P - 2M + P = 34

Then, substitute the value of B in the 1st equation into the 2nd one.

⇒ M + P = 34 → 3P - 2 + P = 34

                               4P - 2 = 34

                                     4P = 34 + 2

                                     4P = 36

                                       P = 36 ÷ 4

                                       P = 9                         ∴ Paul is 9 years old

Now that we found out that Paul's age is 9, we can easily substitute the value into any of the equations. Let's use the 2nd one as it is easier.

⇒ M + P = 34

         M = 34 - 9

         M = 25                                                   ∴ Mary is 25 years old
