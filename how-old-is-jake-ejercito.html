6. Challenge: 4. The sum of the ages of Ron and Jake is 29 years. If Jake is 1 2/3 years older than Ron, how old is each boy?​


Answer:

Ron's age is 41/3 years old, while Jake's age is 46/3 years old

Step-by-step explanation:

Let Ron's age in years be R. Then, Jake's age becomes R + 1 and 2/3 or R + 5/3, and the sum of their ages becomes

[tex]R+J=29[/tex]

[tex]\displaystyle R+\left(R+\dfrac{5}{3}\right)=29[/tex]

[tex]\displaystyle 2R+\frac{5}{3}=29[/tex]

Subtract 5/3 to both sides and isolate R upon division.

[tex]\displaystyle 2R+\frac{5}{3}-\frac{5}{3}=29-\dfrac{5}{3}[/tex]

[tex]2R=\dfrac{82}{3}[/tex]

[tex]\displaystyle R=\frac{\frac{82}{3}}{2}=\frac{41}{3}[/tex]

Finally, substitute the value of R to get their ages[tex]\boxed{\begin{array}{l} \displaystyle \text{Ron's age}= R=\frac{41}{3} \text{ years old} \\ \\ \displaystyle \text{Jake's age}= R+\frac{4}{3}= \frac{41}{3}+\frac{5}{3}=\frac{46}{3} \text{ years old}\end{array}}[/tex]

and that's the answer
