13. let x represent the regular price of a bookC) compute (f •g)(x) and (g• f)(x).descibe what these mean.whic of the these give a better deal for the customer​


f(x) = x - 100

g(x) = 0.9x

(f o g)(x) is a better deal

Step-by-step explanation:

From the given we can define the two functions:

f(x) = x - 100

g(x) = (1 - 0.1)(x) = 0.9x

(f o g)(x) = f(g(x)

f(g(x)) = f(0.9x) = 0.9x - 100

(g o f)(x) = g(f(x)

g(f(x)) = g(x - 100) = 0.9(x - 100) = 0.9x - 90

Since the first composition subtracts more from the price, that means (f o g)(x) is a better deal for the customer.
