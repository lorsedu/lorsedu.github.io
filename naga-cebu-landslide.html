3. What is the impact of naga city landslide?


Answer:

On 20 September 2018, a massive landslide devastated Naga City, Cebu. The Naga City Landslide claimed the lives of 78 villagers and injured 18 while six people remained missing and are presumed dead. The majority of the fatalities were recovered at the landslide toe about 1.2 km from the 200 meter-high headscarp.

Answer:

On September 20, 2018, a landslide caused by heavy rainfall and quarrying operations by Apo Land and Quarry Corporations (ALQC) in Naga, Cebu, Philippines, killed 78 people.

The city is home to a variety of cultural attractions, from ancient monuments and historical sites to modern art galleries and performance spaces. Naga City’s culture is heavily influenced by its long history and its diverse population, with influences from Spanish colonialism, Chinese immigration, and other nearby cultures. Traditional festivals and ceremonies, such as the Sinulog Festival, are held year-round, bringing the city’s vibrant culture to life. The city is also home to many art galleries and performance spaces, where live music, theater, and dance performances are held regularly. Naga City is also home to a variety of unique cuisines, from regional specialties to international flavors, which can be enjoyed throughout the city. With its rich culture, vibrant atmosphere, and unique attractions, Naga City is an ideal destination for anyone looking to explore the cultural heritage of the Philippines.
