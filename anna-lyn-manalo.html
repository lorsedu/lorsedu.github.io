9. ∆ENY ≅ ∆LYN can be proved through what postulate? *


Answer:

The postulate that would apply in this case is the Side-Angle-Side (SAS) postulate.

The SAS postulate states that if two sides and the included angle of one triangle are congruent to two sides and the included angle of another triangle, then the triangles are congruent.

In the case of ∆ENY and ∆LYN, if we can show that one side of each triangle is congruent, the angle between those sides is congruent, and the other side is congruent, then we can use the SAS postulate to conclude that the two triangles are congruent.

So, if we have:

EN ≅ LN (one side is congruent)

∠EYN ≅ ∠LYN (the included angle is congruent)

EY ≅ YL (the other side is congruent)

Then, we can conclude that ∆ENY ≅ ∆LYN by the SAS postulate.
