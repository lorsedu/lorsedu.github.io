3. Reclamation of some parts of Manila Bay, will cause severe floods in coastal areas in Cavite. (Your reason) (Your Evidences)​


Answer:

The reclamation of some parts of Manila Bay could indeed cause severe floods in coastal areas in Cavite due to several reasons.

One reason is that reclamation projects can disrupt the natural flow of water and affect the water catchment capacity of the bay. This can lead to the accumulation of water in coastal areas during heavy rain or typhoons, increasing the risk of flooding.

Another reason is that the reclaimed land can act as a barrier that prevents water from flowing naturally into the bay. As a result, water can accumulate and overflow into nearby areas, causing flooding.

There is also evidence from past experiences that supports this claim. In 2020, during the onslaught of Typhoon Ulysses, the reclamation project in the Manila Bay area was heavily affected. The reclaimed land became a hindrance to the natural flow of water, causing flooding in nearby areas, including Cavite.

Additionally, experts have warned that the reclamation project will lead to the loss of mangrove forests, which serve as natural buffers against storm surges and flooding. Without these natural defenses, the coastal areas in Cavite will be more vulnerable to flooding.

26. reclamation of some parts of manila bay will cause serve floods in coastal area in cavite.​


Explanation:

The reclamation of some parts of Manila Bay can have severe implications on the surrounding coastal areas, particularly in Cavite. The process of reclamation involves filling up coastal areas with sand and other materials, which can have a significant impact on the natural drainage and flow of water in the area. This, in turn, can cause flooding in nearby communities.

Cavite is known to be one of the most flood-prone provinces in the country, and the reclamation project can exacerbate the situation. The loss of natural wetlands and mangroves due to the reclamation can also have an adverse impact on the ecosystem, as these areas serve as natural buffers against flooding and storm surges.

It is essential to consider the long-term consequences of the reclamation project, particularly in terms of its impact on the environment and the communities that surround the area. There needs to be a comprehensive plan that takes into account the potential risks and provides adequate measures to address them.

Alternative solutions should also be explored, such as the rehabilitation of existing coastal areas and the promotion of sustainable development practices. By prioritizing the preservation of the environment and the protection of the communities, we can create a more sustainable and resilient future for everyone.

In conclusion, the reclamation of some parts of Manila Bay can indeed cause severe floods in coastal areas in Cavite. It is crucial to take a holistic and proactive approach to address the issue and ensure that the project's impact is carefully considered and mitigated.
