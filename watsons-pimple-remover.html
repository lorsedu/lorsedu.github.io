Who is Jean Watson? What is Watson's Theory in Nursing?​

Jean Watson was born on June 10, 1940. She is an American nurse theorist and nursing professor who is best known for her book "Transpersonal Caring - The Philosophy and Science of Caring," as well as the theory of Human Caring. Caring is what distinguishes nursing. Nursing at Redlands Community Hospital has incorporated Jean Watson's Caring Science approach. Caring Science assists us in embracing the wonderful energy that comes from a well-balanced mind, body, and spirit, and it benefits both the patient and the nurse in the long run. Nurses are uniquely positioned to be the heart of healing, according to Florence Nightingale's idea that the "job of a nurse is to place her patient in the greatest situation to be able to self-heal." The nurse is able to maximize her patient's potential to heal from within by actively participating in caring via true presence and intentionality. In an overstressed and pressured profession, how can we as nurses preserve emotional sensitivity and caring attitudes? Caring, according to Jean Watson, re-energizes our lives and enhances our skills. The advantages are immense, and they foster personal and professional self-actualization. Both the patient and the nurse, and among the other members of the health team, caring is a mutually rewarding experience. It's also worth remembering that Watson emphasizes the need of taking care of ourselves before we can take care of others; self-healing is a critical step in recharging our spiritual bank and renewing our energy reserves. Make a difference by being the one who makes a difference. It all comes down to what you say and how you say and do it. Our humanity is protected and affirmed by caring. You may live more truly in your relationships if you reveal your actual ideas, feelings, and attitudes. Patients and customers are happier when they are treated with compassion.
