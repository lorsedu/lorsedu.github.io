16. in the history of philippines has there been similar event like the wall street crash and great depression?


During the early half of the 1950s, our country was the model of socio-economic development in East Asia. We were a big industrial player in Asia, ranking second to Japan, we were also expected to continue to rise. We were producing raw materials, consumer goods, pharmace-utical drugs, and industrial parts for automobiles and appliances. Finding a job even without a college degree was easy back then even when people from the provinces relocated to the capital region.

This all changed during the regime of Marcos.

When Marcos was put into power and refused to step down from the presidency, he tried to legitimize and validate Martial Law by borrowing heavily from commercial banks to put into infrastructure and tourism. The problem was the money was not put into efforts to strengthen the agricultural, mining, and manufacturing industry. In fact, many companies had to shut down due to owners fleeing the country or illegal corporate takeovers from Marcos' many cronies. This led to unemployment and less spending.

We were also main exporters of agricultural and mineral products but the focus shifted to manufactured goods. This caused a decline in the value of our agricultural products and with no help from the government to modernize and subsidize the agriculture and mining sector of the country. Over time, manufactured goods from other countries were being made a better quality or less cost, further pushing us to the bottom.

Even after Marcos' reign, the succeeding administration was no better. Due to Aquino's lack of experience, the country was not able to recover.
