Definition:

El Niño: A climate pattern that occurs when the surface water temperature in the eastern Pacific Ocean becomes warmer than usual, leading to changes in atmospheric circulation and weather patterns around the world.
La Niña: A climate pattern that occurs when the surface water temperature in the eastern Pacific Ocean becomes cooler than usual, leading to changes in atmospheric circulation and weather patterns around the world.
CAUSE:

El Niño: An abnormal warming of the ocean surface in the eastern Pacific Ocean caused by a weakening of the trade winds and changes in ocean currents.
La Niña: An abnormal cooling of the ocean surface in the eastern Pacific Ocean caused by a strengthening of the trade winds and changes in ocean currents.
EFFECT:

El Niño: Changes in weather patterns around the world, including droughts, floods, and storms. It can also impact fisheries, agriculture, and human health.
La Niña: Changes in weather patterns around the world, including colder temperatures, increased rainfall in some areas, and droughts in others. It can also impact fisheries, agriculture, and human health.

➡ EL NIÑO ⬅

El Niño means Little Boy, or Christ Child in Spanish. South American fishermen first noticed periods of unusually warm water in the Pacific Ocean in the 1600s. The full name they used was El Niño de Navidad, because El Niño typically peaks around December. El Niño can affect our weather significantly.

➡ EL NIÑA ⬅

La Niña is a weather pattern that occurs in the Pacific Ocean. In this pattern, strong winds blow warm water at the ocean's surface from South America to Indonesia. As the warm water moves west, cold water from the deep rises to the surface near the coast of South America.

14. Ano ang EI Niño at LA Niña?


Answer:

Niño is a term for the warming phase of the El Niño Southern Oscillation (ENSO), a cyclical weather pattern that influences temperature and rainfall across the global. It is a warming of the central to eastern tropical Pacific Ocean. During an El Niño event, sea surface temperatures across the Pacific can warm by 1–3°F or more for anything between a few months to two years. El Niño impacts weather systems around the globe, triggering predictable disruptions in temperature, rainfall and winds.

La Niña is the opposite – a cooling phase of ENSO that tends to have global climate impacts opposite to those of El Niño.

23. Effects of la niña and el niño


El Niño and La Niña are two extreme climate phenomena that can bring devastating effects to lives and the environment itself. Let's discuss them one by one.

El Niño means "little boy child" in Latin. This is a phenomena described with extreme heat and low precipitation. Its effects are:

Drought. El Niño brings about drought which can damage plantations on the places that it wreaks havoc to.Wildfires. Extreme temperatures dry up grasses and can ultimately cause wildfires. This can burn down several hectares of forest in just a few days. Health Concerns. Extreme heat can cause dehydration not only to plants but also to humans and animals. Animals and humans can suffer heat stroke when conditions are dire and water is not available.

La Niña means "little girl child" in Latin. This is described as the extended periods of heavy rains and storms. This consequences of this are the following:

Flooding. Continuous rains for days can fill dams and thus water needs to be released. This can flood the low-lying areas near the dam. The same applies to cities with poor drainage systems.Landslides. Continuous rains can soften the slopes, causing it to erode and eventually destroy a portion of the mountain.Health Concerns. Flooding can cause health alerts for diseases like leptospirosis due to poor drainage system where rats can reside. The bacteria that causes leptospirosis comes from the urine of rats.
