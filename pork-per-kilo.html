26. Pedro's income in January was 1000 pesos per week. in Februar. his income increased for pork from 1 kilo to 1.5 kilos. determine the income elasticity for pork and interpret your answer.​


Answer:

Let's start by finding the percentage change in Pedro's income from January to February:

Percentage change in income = [(February income - January income) / January income] x 100%

February income = 1000 pesos per week x 4 weeks = 4000 pesos

Percentage change in income = [(4000 - 4000) / 4000] x 100% = 0%

Pedro's income did not change from January to February, so the percentage change in income is 0.

Now, let's find the percentage change in the quantity of pork Pedro consumes:

Percentage change in quantity = [(February quantity - January quantity) / January quantity] x 100%

January quantity = 1 kilo per week x 4 weeks = 4 kilos

February quantity = 1.5 kilos per week x 4 weeks = 6 kilos

Percentage change in quantity = [(6 - 4) / 4] x 100% = 50%

The percentage change in the quantity of pork Pedro consumes is 50%.

Now we can calculate the income elasticity of pork for Pedro:

Income elasticity = (Percentage change in quantity / Percentage change in income)

Income elasticity = (50% / 0%)

Since the denominator is zero, the income elasticity is undefined. This means that the quantity of pork Pedro consumes is not responsive to changes in his income.

Interpretation: The income elasticity of pork for Pedro is undefined, indicating that the quantity of pork he consumes is not affected by changes in his income. This means that pork is an income-inelastic good for Pedro.
