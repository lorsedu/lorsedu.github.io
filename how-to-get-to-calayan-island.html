1. ust to refresh your geography knowledge, Babuyan has five major inhabited islands, namely: Fuga, Dalupiri, Camiguin, Calayan, and the Babuyan Island (its namesake; also called Babuyan Claro or Curuga-Babuyan)


Answer:

Five Main Islands of the Babuyan Group

These are the five main islands of the Babuyan Group of Islands:

Babuyan IslandCalayan IslandCamiguin IslandDalupiri IslandFuga Island

Explanation:

The Babuyan Group of Islands is a volcanic chain of active and inactive volcanoes. The island of Babuyan and Camiguin Island are home to three active volcanoes, which are Babuyan Claro, Camiguin de Babuyanes, and Smith. Didicas and Iraya are also found in the same volcanic chain, but they are not found on the five main island. Instead, Iraya is found in Batanes, while Didicas is found northeast of Camiguin Island. Calayan, Dalupiri, and Fuga Islands have extinct volcanoes, and they are also popular with tourists because of their beautiful sceneries.

Answer:

Classification of Volcanoes: Active or Inactive

Here are the correct answers:

Didicas Volcano – ACTIVEKanlaon Volcano – ACTIVECalayan Volcano – INACTIVEMount Bulusan – ACTIVECanipo Volcano – INACTIVEMount Bontes – INACTIVEMount Banahaw – ACTIVEMount Hibok-hibok – ACTIVEBiliran Volcano – ACTIVEMount Burgos - INACTIVE

Explanation:

The active volcanoes on the list above are Didicas Volcano, Kanlaon Volcano, Mount Bulusan, Mount Banahaw, Mount Hibok-hibok, and Biliran Volcano. Didicas Volcano last erupted in 1952, and it is located in the Babuyan Group of Islands. Kanlaon Volcano, found in the island of Negros, is the 3rd most active volcano in the Philippines. Mount Bulusan in Sorsogon is the 4th most active volcano in the country. Mount Banahaw is a complex volcano in the borders of Laguna and Quezon. Mount Hibok-hibok last erupted in 1951, and it is located in Camiguin. Mount Biliran, in Biliran province, is also a complex volcano, which last erupted in 1939.
