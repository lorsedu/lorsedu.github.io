1. advocacy of narayanan krishnan


Answer:

Narayanan Krishnan is an Indian who was born 1981 in Madurai, Tamil Nadu.

In the year 2002, he is already one of the best chefs in their time. Considering that he is a Brahmin – an upper class Hindu – destined for a successful career as a chef.

He was about to work on a 5-star hotel in Switzerland. And right before he went to the country, he decided to go home first in Madurai to visit his parents.

When he was on the way to Madurai, he saw an old man eating his own human waste for his food. With this horrible sight, Narayanan thought to himself that he should help these kind of people. Which became his advocacy.

He established the Akshaya Trust organization in 2003. A a nonprofit organization through which he has already served over one and a half million meals to India’s homeless.

He also provides haircuts and shaves to give dignity to those he serves and he was selected as one of the Top 10 in "CNN heroes 2010" list.

14. ﻿What is the nationality and advocacy of Ghalib Khalil,Efren Peñaflorida,Mark Hyman, Arnel Pineda,Evans Wadongo ,Narayanan Krishnan,Julia ‘Butterfly’ Hill.


Ghalib Khalil

A Pakistani teenager who founded the Rescue Pakistan Youth Foundation. Ghalib Khalil’s advocacy is to make a positive change in his country by improving the lives of the youth and giving them hope so they can become movers of their country in the future. He has received several recognitions, one of the most notable is from the Youth Service America as one of the 25 Most Powerful and Influential Young People in the World. He was also nominated for an Agahi Award and won a Commonwealth Photographic Award for his photos that capture the difficult life in his country.

Efren Peñaflorida

The nationality of Efren Peñaflorida, Jr. is Filipino, and his whose advocacy is to spread education and reforming children in the slums of the Cavite, where his advocacy started. He was then nominated as CNN Hero of the Year. He has helped 150,000 children and has about 10,000 youth volunteers. His approach was quite unorthodox: he uses a kariton that carries reading materials and other daily necessities to teach children not only how to read but also how to maintain their hygiene.  

Mark Hyman

Dr. Mark Hyman is a modern-day doctor who believes that everyone deserves a life of vitality and that this is possible for us to make it ourselves. He looks into the root causes of chronic diseases by harnessing Functional Medicine to transform healthcare. He is an advocate of Complementary and Alternative medicine. He believes in lifestyle change and faith-based cure to treat chronic illnesses. Dr. Hyman is the Medical Director of Cleveland Clinic’s Center for Functional Medicine, and is the Founder of The UltraWellness Center. He is also a ten-time #1 New York Times Bestselling author.

Arnel Pineda

Arnel Pineda is a Filipino artist who, apart from being the new vocalist of the Journey, once struggled with homelessness and hunger in the streets of Manila. This experience has brought him to establish the Arnel Pineda Foundation, Inc. (APFI), which advocates to help street children survive.  

Evans Wadongo

Evans Wadongo is from Kenya. At the age of 19, he developed the first African-designed and first produced solar lamp. Instead of commercializing his work, he used it to give light to his community. His creation is called “Good Light” or MwangaBora. He was named as one of CNN’s Top Ten Heroes. He leads the Sustainable Development for All-Kenya, which advocates sustainable development for his country. The Schwab Foundation also named him Social Entrepreneur of the Year, while he is in the list of Esquire magazine’s "20 Men Who Will Shape the Next 20 Years."

Narayanan Krishnan

Narayanan Krishnan’s advocacy is to help homeless, poor, and mentally ill people in India. He is an Indian social worker and philanthropist named as CNN Heroes of the Year in 2010. He is deep into his service that he founded the Akshaya Trust, which focuses on feeding the poor and the mentally ill. He even gives them haircuts and shelter.  

Julia ‘Butterfly’ Hill

Julia “Butterfly” Hill is an American activist known for staying Luna, a 1,500-year-old redwood tree, for 738 days to prevent loggers from cutting it down. All efforts of Maxxam Corporation, the operator of Pacific Lumber, to get her off the tree was in vain. Hill was successful in her advocacy as Luna remains standing to date. Hill wrote the book “The Legacy of Luna,” which became a bestseller. This is to further her advocacy for protecting the environment—the trees particularly. She established the Circle of Life Foundation and helped set up other organizations like Engage Network, What's Your Tree, We The Planet, and Activism Is Patriotism. She was recognized by The California State History Museum in the "California's Remarkable Women" exhibit and in Georgemagazine, as one of the 20 most influential women. She is also the youngest person elected to the Ecology Hall of Fame.
