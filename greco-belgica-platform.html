9. Difference between roman and greco art to early christian art


Answer:

The Early Christian arts focus is on a narrative and its symbolic style enhances that narrative. Early Christian painting had a more flattened space than the three dimensional look of Roman painting. Stylistically, Roman painting showed a more imaginary world than the Early Christians did.

Answer:

The shift from polytheism to monotheism would account for the similarities and differences between Roman and Early Christian art. The reasons for the similarities between the two art forms would be that the Early Christians borrowed stylistically from the Romans. Another might be that the Early Christians wanted to avoid all forms of persecution.

Early Christians, having recently been persecuted, first met literally underground in catacombs. Because the Early Christian’s artwork looked similar to the Roman’s artwork, it would not arouse suspicion of anyone that opposed their beliefs. The images looked ambiguous. They take on new meaning, depending on the viewer’s personal religious beliefs. For example: “Orant figures – worshipers with arms outstretched in prayer- can be pagan, Jewish, or Christian”. (Stokstad & Cothren, pg. 222)

The Early Christian arts focus is on a narrative and its symbolic style enhances that narrative. Early Christian painting had a more flattened space than the three dimensional look of Roman painting. Stylistically, Roman painting showed a more imaginary world than the Early Christians did.

Early Christian wall painting was similar to the Roman wall painting in the fluidity of brush strokes. Also similar to Roman art, the Early Christian ceiling fresco on the catacomb ceiling of St. Peter and Marcellinus, Christ is shown beardless and youthful – Greco-Roman in appearance. (Stokstad & Cothren, pg. 225)

Different from Roman art, Early Christian art often has no setting for it’s figures. They appear floating on a colored background. Early Christian art narratives in paintings show figures multiple times. For example: detail of the wall painting “Moses Pulled from the Bull rushes”. (Bowen, lecture #10) Moses’ figure is shown multiple times within the narrative. Roman paintings do not use the same figure multiple times.

Early Christian narratives show the same composition as Roman narratives. For example: the Sarcophagus of Junius Bassus (Stokstad & Cothren, pg. 230) top center “Christ Giving of the Law”. Christ is shown as a new emperor, looking like a young philosopher, like the Roman rulers would be shown. He is seated in an elevated position in the center of the composition, just as important Roman rulers were depicted. Peter and Paul are standing on either side of him, as would important people in Roman society would surround their ruler or king. Early Christian symbology is shown by Christ having his foot rest (or push down) on top of a polytheistic god. This symbolizes Christianity’s triumph over polytheism and Christ as the ruler or king of mankind. The style of this carving has a classical Roman look. The clothing draperies carved in the Roman style and the figures look like they are Roman people.

Kontribusyon:

2. Emperador Cluadius (August 01, 10 BC - October 13, 54 AD)
Ang ikaapat na Emperador ng Roma ng Dinastiyang Julio-Claudian na namuno mula Enero 24,41 hanggang sa kanyang kamatayan noong 54.

Kontribusyon
Nilikha niya ang isang burukrasya na binubuo ng mga batikang administrador.

3. Vespian (Nov. 17, 9 AD - June 23, 79 AD)
Mula sa Dinastiyang Flavian
Kontribusyon
Kinilala ang dinastiyang Flavian sa maayos na patakarang pananalapi at pagtatayo ng imprastrakturang paliguan at amphitheater para sa mga labanan ng mga gladiator.

4. Hadrian (January 24, 76 AD - July 10, 138 AD)
Anak ng isang ex-pretor.

Isinilang noong Enero 24, 76.

Kontribusyon
Siya ay kilala sa kanyang maraming proyekto sa pagtatayo, mga lungsod na pinangalanang Hadrianopolis(Adrianopolis), ant ang bantog na pader sa buong Britanya, mula Tyne hanggang Solway, na dinisenyo upang mapanatili ang mga barbarian sa labas ng Romano Britanya.

5. Marcus Aurelius (Abril 26, 121- Marso 17, 180)
Siya ay isang scholar at manunulat.

Siya ang huli sa Limang Mabubuting mga Emperador.

Kontribusyon
Itinaguyod nya ang pilosopiyang Stoic. Binibigyang diin ng pilosopiyang ito ang paghahanap ng kaligayahan sa pamamagitan ng pamumuhay ayon sa banal na kalooban.

6. Virgil ( Oktubre 15, 70BKE - 19BKE)
Mas kilala bilang Virgilio o Vergil, ay isang sinaunang makatang Romano.

Kontribusyon
Siya ay nakilala sa kanyang tatlong pangunahing akda sa panitikang Latin, ang Ecloga o Eclogues (Mga Pili o Mga Piniling Sipi) o Buocolics (Buhay ng Isang Pastol).

Ang Eclogues o Ecloga ay isang pangkat ng sampung maromansang mga tula na naglalaman ng mga pag-ibig at pighati ng mga pastol ng tupa at kambing, na may hindi makatotohanan ngunit kabigha-bighaning kayarian.

Georgica (Georgics, Buhay sa Bukid) ay isang seryoso at makatotohanang tula pagkatapos ng Ecloga, na binuo ng apat na aklat tungkol sa mga gawain ng isang magsasaka.

Aeneis (Ang Salaysay ukol kay Aeneas) ang pinakahuling akda, na naglalaman ng isang kuwento tungkol sa pakikipagsapalarang pangbayaning mag labindalawang bahagi o aklat.

7. Livy (59 BCE - 17 CE)
Isang Romanong manunulat ng kasaysayan na sumulat ng isang mahalagang kasaysayan ng Roma at ng mga taong Romano.

Kontribusyon
Sinulat nya ang Ab Urbe Condita Libi o Mga aklat mula sa Pagkatatag ng Lungsod, na sumasaklaw sa kapanahunan magmula sa pinakamaagang mga alamat ng Roma bago pa man ang pangtradisyong pagtatag noong 753 BCE.

28. What is Greco Roman art explain in short?


Answer:

pertaining to or designating a style of the fine arts developed in Rome or the Roman Empire from the middle of the 1st century b.c. to the early 4th century a.d., chiefly characterized by an apparent indebtedness to Greek forms or motifs modified by technological innovation, monumental scale, the combination of symbolic with narrative treatment of subject matter, and an emphasis on the commemorative aspect of a work of art.


29. Ano ang sanhi at bunga ng Greco Persian War


Greco-Persian Wars, also called Persian Wars, (492–449 bce), series of wars fought by Greek states and Persia over a period of almost half a century.

The fighting was most intense during two invasions that Persia launched against mainland Greece between 490 and 479.
