18. 3) A motorcycle breaks down and the rider has to walk the rest of the way to work. The motorcycle was travelling at 45 kph, and the rider walks at speed of 6 kph. The distance from home to work is 25 kilometers and the total time trip was 2 hours. How far did the motorcycle go before it broke down? Representation:


Answer:

Let x be the distance travelled by the motorcycle before it broke down.

The time taken by the motorcycle to travel the distance x is given by x/45.

After the motorcycle broke down, the rider had to walk the remaining distance of (25 - x) kilometers at a speed of 6 kph. The time taken by the rider to cover this distance is (25 - x)/6.

The total time taken for the trip is given as 2 hours, so we can write the equation:

Step-by-step explanation:

x/45 + (25 - x)/6 = 2

Multiplying both sides by the LCM of 270, we get:

6x + 45(25 - x) = 540

Simplifying the equation, we get:

6x + 1125 - 45x = 540

-39x = -585

x = 15

Therefore, the motorcycle travelled 15 kilometers before it broke down.
