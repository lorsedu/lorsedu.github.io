11. what is your take on the governments decision to pattern our opening of school to that of the other countries? Should we go back to the month of june as our opening of classes? why or why not? essay​


Answer:

The decision to pattern the opening of schools in a country to that of other countries can have both advantages and disadvantages. On the one hand, it can help ensure consistency in the education system and facilitate the exchange of ideas and best practices among different countries. It can also help to align academic calendars and make it easier for students who travel or move to different countries.

On the other hand, there are several factors to consider when deciding whether to change the traditional school calendar. For example, the climate and weather patterns in a particular region can have an impact on the school calendar. In some countries, schools close during the hottest months of the year, while in others, they close during the coldest months. Changing the school calendar can also affect the availability of teachers and students, as well as the overall academic performance of the students.

In the Philippines, the traditional school calendar starts in June and ends in March, with a two-month summer break. However, in 2020, due to the COVID-19 pandemic, the government decided to move the start of the school year to October to give schools more time to prepare for remote learning. The decision was met with mixed reactions from educators, parents, and students.

There are arguments for and against returning to the traditional June opening of classes. Some argue that it is important to maintain consistency in the education system and align with other countries. Others argue that the traditional school calendar is not well-suited to the Philippine climate, which can result in low attendance rates and poor academic performance during the rainy season.

Ultimately, the decision to change the school calendar should be based on a thorough analysis of the benefits and drawbacks of each option. It should also take into consideration the views and opinions of all stakeholders, including educators, parents, and students.

27. opening remarks speech about nutrition month 2018


Opening remarks for nutrition month 2018 / Talking points nutrition month 2018:


Good day, everyone!


I welcome you all to our celebration for 2018’s nutrition month!


The National Nutrition Council or NNC for short was actually the national agency or council that was tasked to help encourage, promote, and support greater awareness of the significance of nutrition among Filipinos in our country, Philippines.  


In fact, the Nutrition Month theme this 2018 is “Ugaliing magtanim, Sapat na nutrisyon aanihin!” and this might be the best slogan for a nutrition month celebration.

 

The said theme or slogan for nutrition month here in the Philippines apparently aims to improve the Filipino’s healthy food consumption as well as their food security. Also, this year’s objective is for the citizens to be self sufficient as much as possible especially with updating knowledge when it comes to backyard gardening and agriculture.  


I would also want to add the fact that this theme or slogan of nutrition month perhaps advocates protecting our Philippine’s ancestral lands as well as its peoples, the indigenous people like the Lumads, Aetas, etc.


For more information about the NNC, this council was created by Presidential Decree 491 during 1974 and it is acting as the country’s highest policy making as well as coordinating body on nutrition in our country. Such organization is chaired by the secretary of health and the board must be composed of ten government organizations. The organizations are the following: Department of Health, Department of Agriculture, Department of Interior and Local Government, Department of Trade and Industry, Department of Labor and Employment, Department of Social Welfare and Development, Department of Science and Technology, Department of Budget Management, National Economic Development Authority and most of all Department of Education.  


So here we are, celebrating nutrition for the whole month with an advocacy to not just be healthy Filipinos but also wise when it comes to agriculture and livelihood.

Write a short intro or introduction, one to two paragraphs for the body of your speech, and then make a short conclusion. Make sure you have energy for the welcome address or opening remarks! If you start it with low energy, that unpleasant vibes will continue 'til end of the program. Oh, also, don't make jokes that are not appropriate for the program or event... especially for kids in school. You may search or surf the internet for more tips!
