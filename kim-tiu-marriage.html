1. Compare and contrast the culture of Vietnam as revealed in the story by kim tao hai with the Filipinos’ beliefs and practices regarding marriage.


Answer:

Vietnam and the Philippines both have similar beliefs and practices when it comes to marriage. In both cultures, marriage is traditionally viewed as a union between two families, rather than two individuals. Both cultures also practice arranged marriages, where the parents of the bride and groom will negotiate the marriage arrangements.

However, there are some differences between the two cultures. In Vietnam, the bride and groom are expected to follow the wishes of their parents and elders. In the Philippines, the bride and groom have more autonomy and can choose to reject their parents’ arrangements if they wish. Additionally, in Vietnam, the bride and groom may not meet each other before their wedding day, whereas in the Philippines, it is more common for the couple to meet and get to know each other before the wedding.

In Vietnam, marriage is traditionally seen as a union between families rather than just between two individuals. It is often arranged by the parents and the couple may not have much say in the matter. Divorce is also not as accepted in Vietnam as it is in other countries. Additionally, it is common for the bride to move in with the groom's family and take on the role of caring for her in-laws.

In the Philippines, marriage is traditionally a Catholic ceremony and is seen as a union between two individuals. Divorce is not legal in the Philippines, but annulment is possible. It is also common for the couple to live with the wife's family after marriage.

In Vietnam, there is also a strong emphasis on having children, especially sons, and a large family is seen as a sign of success. In the Philippines, having children is also seen as important, but the emphasis is not as strong.

Overall, the culture of Vietnam and Philippines differs on how marriage is viewed, arranged, and the role of the couple and their families in the marriage.

Rio: x
Kim: x

New amount of money with the ratio of 2:3.

Rio: x - 300   (gave 300 to Kim)
Kim: x + 300 (add 300 the amount from Rio)

Solution:

(x - 300) : (x + 300) = 2:3

Equate the product of means to the product of extremes:

2 (x+300) = 3 (x - 300)
2x + 600 = 3x - 900
3x - 2x = 600 + 900
x = 1,500

ANSWER: The original amount of money each has is 1,500 pesos.

Answer:

"Ang Bata Nga Dili Matulog" is a Cebuano short story by Macariu Tiu that tells the story of a young boy who struggles to fall asleep at night. Despite his mother's efforts to comfort him, the boy remains wide awake, and his mind begins to wander. He reflects on his day, his fears, and his dreams, until eventually, he drifts off to sleep. The story explores the theme of childhood anxiety and the importance of parental support in helping children overcome their fears.
