11. Five years ago Mrs. Cruz was four times as old as her daughter. Now, she is only three times as old. Find their present ages.


Answer:

Let x be the present age of the daughter.

Five years ago, the daughter's age was x - 5.

Five years ago, Mrs. Cruz's age was 4 times the age of her daughter:

Mrs. Cruz's age 5 years ago = 4(x - 5)

Mrs. Cruz's present age is 5 more than her age 5 years ago:

Mrs. Cruz's present age = 4(x - 5) + 5

According to the problem, Mrs. Cruz is now three times as old as her daughter, so:

Mrs. Cruz's present age = 3x

Setting the two expressions for Mrs. Cruz's present age equal to each other, we get:

4(x - 5) + 5 = 3x

Simplifying and solving for x, we get:

4x - 15 = 3x - 5

x = 10

Therefore, the daughter's present age is 10, and Mrs. Cruz's present age is 3x = 30.
