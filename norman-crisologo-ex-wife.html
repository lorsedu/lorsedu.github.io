7. line calle crisologo​


The Philippines has a lot of places that stood the test of time and served as witnesses to many historical events, dating back from the pre-colonial period to the freedom wars. One of those is Vigan where we can see the famous Calle Crisologo.

Calle Crisologo is a street of old Spanish structures, mostly houses of wealthy families and Filipino-Chinese traders. This is where you’ll see the famous cobblestone streets that represent Vigan’s image.

Unknown to many, before the name Calle Crisologo street, it was called Calle Escolta de Vigan upon the discovery of the town by Juan de Salcedo in 1572. The street served as the commercial hub in the entire Northern Luzon and was established even before the Galleon Trade.

Calle Crisologo was also called “Kasanglayan” (place of the Chinese), which was the business center of the foreign migratory traders including Chinese, Spanish, Mexican, Japanese and other European businessmen.

Unknown to many, before the name Calle Crisologo street, it was called Calle Escolta de Vigan upon the discovery of the town by Juan de Salcedo in 1572. The street served as the commercial hub in the entire Northern Luzon and was established even before the Galleon Trade.

In the late 1890s, the Calle de Escolta De Vigan was renamed to Washington Street during the American rule. On September 1, 1901, the Americans had established a civil government in Ilocos Sur with Don Mena Crisologo, a delegate to the Malolos Congress, as the first provincial governor.

Crisologo was highly revered by Ilocanos as a writer and politician. After retiring from government service, he died on July 5, 1927 at the age of 82. Following Crisologo’s death, Washington Street was renamed again to Mena Crisologo Street in honor of the late governor.

Through the years, Mena Crisologo Street or now known as Calle Crisologo became famous as it served as the center of well-preserved houses and remains as the main attraction in Veegan.
