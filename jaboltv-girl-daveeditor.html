[tex]\large\color{skyblue}{ \boxed{\tt{Answer:}}}[/tex][tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\sf \color{lightgreen}d.40 \: girls \\ \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf solution : [/tex]

[tex] \sf5: 8 = 65 \\ \\ \sf 5n + 8n = 13 \\ \sf65 = 13 \\ \\ \sf \frac{65}{13} \div \frac{13n}{13} = 5n \\ \\ \sf5 \times 5 = 25 \\ \sf5 \times 8 = \color{lightgreen}40 \\ \\ \sf25n + 40n = 65 \\ \sf 25 : 40 \\ \\ \\ [/tex]

#CarryOnLearning

Answer:

If the ratio of boys to girls is 5:8, and the total of the whole class is 65, then there are 40 girls in the class because there are 25 boys.

Explanation:

"We see in this question that the ratio of boys to girls is 5:8, and the total of the class is 65, so, to find this, we first need to add the 5 and 8 together, and that would give us 13. Next, we would need to divide 65 by 13 because 13 is the sum of our ratio, and 65 is the sum of the ratio of boys in girls, where we would need to analyze the specific amount of boys and girls based on their total. So, 65 / 13 is equal to 5, which means we would need to multiply the numbers in our ratio to 5 to find our specific ratio which would give the total of 65. So, 5 (5:8) is equal to 25:40, where 25 is the number of boys and 40 is the number of girls."

Solution:

> 5:8 = n1:n2

> n1 + n2 = 65

> 5 + 8 = 13

> 65 / 13 = 5

> 5 (5 : 8) = 25 : 40

> n1 = 25

> n2 = 40

> 25 + 40 = 65

Final Answer: 25:40

> 25 Boys

> 40 Girls

» Let x be multiplied to each part of the ratio having a total of 40.

[tex] 3x + 5x = 40 [/tex]

[tex] 8x = 40 [/tex]

[tex] \frac{8x}{8} = \frac{40}{8}\\ [/tex]

[tex] x = 5 [/tex]

» The value of x is 5. Multiply it to the part of the ratio for the girls to determine how many girls are there.

[tex] Girls = 5x [/tex]

[tex] Girls = 5(5) [/tex]

[tex] Girls = 25 [/tex]
