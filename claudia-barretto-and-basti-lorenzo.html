5. Claudia dumped her 200-penny coin collection on the floor and counted the number of pennies that landed heads up. Claudia repeated this process 5 times and had an average of 84 pennies landing heads up on each try. Which of the following statements is true? If Claudia had repeated this process fewer times, the average number of pennies landing heads up would be closer to 100. If Claudia had repeated this process more times, the average number of pennies landing heads up would be closer to 100. Each penny has a greater probability of landing heads up than tails up. The theoretical probability of a penny landing heads up is 21/50.


Answer:

If Claudia had repeated this process more times, the average number of pennies landing heads up would be closer to 100.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let us evaluate each of the statements thoroughly instead of simply choosing one answer in order to properly understand why it is true or false.

A) If Claudia had repeated this process fewer times, the average number of pennies landing heads up would be closer to 100.

The given is what gives us what we call the experimental values, this is what we got based on performing the action. Out of 200 coins, 84 landed on heads for all the 5 trials.

However, in reality, each side of the coin has exactly the same chance of being landed on. 50% for heads and the same for tails. Thus, out of 200 coins, the theoretical value is that 100 would land on heads.

Lessening the number of trials would mean that we have less to work with and based on the given, it would more or less result to 84 still. It would NOT go closer to 100.

So FALSE.

B) If Claudia had repeated this process more times, the average number of pennies landing up would be closer to 100.

As stated above, each side of the coin really has equal chance of being landed on. Thus, the more trials we perform, the more evident this fact becomes and the experimental will approach the theoretical. In other words, the average number landing on heads would indeed be closer to 100.

So TRUE.

C) Each penny has a greater probability of landing heads up than tails up.

Considering we are dealing with regular coins, the probability of landing on heads or tails is exactly the same.

So, FALSE.

D) The theoretical probability of a penny landing heads up is 21/50.

Again, the two sides of a regular coin have equal chances of landing on heads or tales. So instead of 21/50, it should rather be 25/50 or 1/2.

So, FALSE.

Therefore, only the second statement is true.

24. who is Lorenzo monaco ?


Answer:

Lorenzo Monaco (born Piero di Giovanni; c. 1370 – c. 1425) was an Italian painter of the late Gothic-early Renaissance age. He was born Piero di Giovanni in Siena.

In 1390 he joined the Camaldolese monastery of Santa Maria degli Angeli. He was thenceforth generally known as Lorenzo Monaco (English: "Lawrence the Monk").

In 1414 he painted the Coronation of the Virgin (now at the Uffizi), characterized by a great number of saints and brilliant colours.


25. Write a reflection of Saint Lorenzo​


Saint Lorenzo was a Christian deacon and martyr who lived in the 3rd century AD. He is venerated as a saint by the Catholic and Orthodox Churches.

According to tradition, Saint Lorenzo was serving as a deacon in Rome during the persecution of Christians under the Roman Emperor Valerian. He was arrested and sentenced to death by being roasted on a gridiron, which became one of his most commonly depicted symbols.

Saint Lorenzo is remembered for his bravery and faith in the face of persecution and suffering. His feast day is celebrated on August 10th, and he is the patron saint of cooks and librarians.

His legacy has inspired many works of art, including paintings, sculptures, and stained glass windows. He serves as a reminder of the importance of standing up for one's beliefs and the power of faith in difficult times.
