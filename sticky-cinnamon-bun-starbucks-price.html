2. Why are prices sticky? price changes are not easy to implement because?


There are many factors that can affect prices to be sticky. Some are:

- cost to update pricing; this includes changing marketing materials to reflect the new price
- inaccurate information in the market
- a decision from the CEO to keep the pricing despite its drawbacks
- being engaged in a long-term contract

The buns are sold by the dozen, and each dozen is sold for the same price. On Friday, the bakery collected $360 from selling 45 loaves of bread and 30 dozen buns. On Saturday, the bakery collected $470 from selling 65 loaves of bread and 35 dozen buns. At what price does the bakery sell each loaf of bread?


The bakery sells each loaf of bread for $ 4.00.

Check:

Friday

45 loaves of bread x $ 4.00 = $ 180.00

30 dozen buns x $ 6.00 = $ 180.00

$ 180.00 + $ 180.00 = $ 360.00

Saturday

65 loaves of bread x $ 4.00 = $ 260.00

35 dozen buns x $ 6.00 = $ 210.00

$ 260.00 + $ 210.00 = $ 470.00
