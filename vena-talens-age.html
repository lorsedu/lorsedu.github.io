5. What are the functions of the superior vena cava and inferior vena cava? How about the aorta?​


Answer:

The inferior vena cava is the lower ("inferior") of the two venae cavae, the two large veins that carry deoxygenated blood from the body to the right atrium of the heart: the inferior vena cava carries blood from the lower half of the body whilst the superior vena cava carries blood from the upper half of the body


6. what are the functions of the superior vena cava and inferior vena cava? ​


Answer:

The inferior vena cava is the lower ("inferior") of the two venae cavae, the two large veins that carry deoxygenated blood from the body to the right atrium of the heart: the inferior vena cava carries blood from the lower half of the body whilst the superior vena cava carries blood from the upper half of the body.

The superior vena cava (SVC) is the superior of the two venae cavae, the great venous trunks that return deoxygenated blood from the systemic circulation to the right atrium of the heart. It is a large-diameter (24 mm) short length vein that receives venous return from the upper half of the body, above the diaphragm.

8. what is inferior vena cava


Inferior Vena Cava

Inferior Vena Cava or IVC is the largest vein in the human body that is located at the posterior abdominal wall on the right side of the aorta. It carries blood from the lower and middle body, into the right atrium of the heart and has rigid walls that has valves so that the blood does not flow down via gravity.

22. function of the vena cava​


The vena cava brings deoxygenated blood from the tissues into the right atrium

Answer:

The inferior vena cava is ultimately responsible for the transport of almost all venous blood (deoxygenated) from the abdomen and lower extremities back to the right side of the heart for oxygenation.

Explanation:

The inferior vena cava (IVC) is the largest vein of the human body. It is located at the posterior abdominal wall on the right side of the aorta. The IVC's function is to carry the venous blood from the lower limbs and abdominopelvic region to the heart.
