shall indemnify the latter for the same.

Article 21. Any person who wilfully causes loss or injury to another in manner that is contrary to

morals, good customs or public policy shall compensate the latter for the damage.

acquires or comes into possession of something at the expense of the latter without just or legal

ground, shall return the same to him.

fault or negligence of the defendant, the latter shall be liable for indemnity if through the act or

event he was benefited.

disadvantage on account of his moral dependence, ignorance, indigence, mental weakness,

tender age or other handicap, the courts must be vigilant for his protection.

acute public want or emergency may be stopped by order of the courts at the instance of any

government or private charitable institution.

9. explain what is philippine electrical code (pec) article 3.14​


Answer:

The primary purpose of the Philippine Electrical Code is to minimize the risk of electricity as a source of electric shock and as a potential ignition source of fires and explosions; and to minimize the propagation of fire and explosions due to electrical installations.

The primary objective of the code is to establish basic materials quality and electrical works standards for the safe use of electricity for light, heat, power, communications, signaling and for other purposes. This code is intended as a design specification or an instruction manual for qualified persons.

15. explain what is philippine electrical code (pec) article 1.3.2​


The Philippine Electrical Code (PEC) is a set of standards and guidelines that govern the design, installation, maintenance, and operation of electrical systems in the Philippines. Article 1.3.2 of the PEC specifically pertains to the scope and application of the code.

Article 1.3.2 states that the PEC shall apply to all electrical systems and installations, including their component parts, within the jurisdiction of the Philippines. This includes electrical systems in buildings, structures, and premises, as well as outdoor installations such as overhead power lines and underground cables.

Furthermore, the PEC applies to all voltages, from low voltage (less than 1000 volts) to high voltage (greater than 1000 volts). The code also covers both alternating current (AC) and direct current (DC) systems.

The purpose of Article 1.3.2 and the PEC as a whole is to promote safety and prevent electrical accidents by ensuring that electrical systems are designed and installed properly, and are maintained and operated in accordance with established standards.

19. The family code of the philippines that we are currently abiding from was drafted from which of the following constitution?


The Family Code of the Philippines  

Based on Executive No. 209, July 6, 1987, with details:

REPUBLIC ACT NO. 8533

AN ACT AMENDING TITLE I, CHAPTER 3, ARTICLE 39 OF EXECUTIVE ORDER NO. 209, OTHERWISE KNOWN AS THE FAMILY CODE OF THE PHILIPPINES, NULLIFIES THE PRESCRIPTIVE PERIOD FOR ACTION OR DEFENSES GROUNDED ON PSYCHOLOGICAL INCAPACITY

Detailed Specifics in General

THE FAMILY

The State recognizes the Filipino family as the nation's bedrock in:

Section 1. Consequently, it will bolster its cohesion and actively encourage its overall development.

Section 2. Marriage, as an inviolable social institution, is the bedrock of the family and shall be safeguarded by the State.

Section 3: The State must:

(1) The right of spouses to establish a family in accordance with their religious beliefs and the responsibilities of responsible parenthood.

(2) The right of children to help, including sufficient care and nutrition, as well as specific protection against all sorts of neglect, abuse, cruelty, exploitation, and other conditions detrimental to their development.

(3) The right of the family to a salary and income sufficient for subsistence; and

(4) The right of families and family organizations to participate in the formulation and implementation of policies and programs affecting them.

(5)The family has the responsibility to care for its older members, but the State may also do so through social security programs.
