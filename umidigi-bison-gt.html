4. Past Perfect Progressive - Private Ryu ( report) to Colonel Guile before he was reassigned to General Bison.​


Answer:

Private Ryu ( report) to Colonel Guile before he was reassigned to General Bison.​

Answer: Private Ryu HAD BEEN REPORTING to Colonel Guile before he was reassigned to General Bison.​

Explanation:

Past Perfect Progressive Tense. The PAST PERFECT PROGRESSIVE TENSE indicates a continuous action that was completed at some point in the past. This tense is formed with the modal "HAD" plus "BEEN," plus the present participle of the verb (with an -ing ending): "I had been working in the garden all morning.

EXAMPLES:

She had been painting the door before the dog scratched it. The jury had been considering its verdict for several hours when the judge effectively ordered them to find Jones guilty.

17. A white-tailed deer can sprint at speeds up to 30 miles per hour. American bison can run at speeds up to 3,520 feet per minute. Which animal is faster and by how many miles per hour? There are 5,280 feet in one mile.


Answer:

[tex]\boxed{The\:American\:bison\:is\:faster\:than\:the\:white\:tailed\;deer\:by\:10\:miles\:per\:hour.}[/tex]

Computation:

Given,

[tex]\left[\begin{array}{ccc}White-tailed\:deer:&30\:mi/hr\\American\:bison:&3,520\:ft/min\\5,280\:ft=&1\:mi\end{array}\right][/tex]

Convert the speed of American bison to miles per hour.

[tex]\frac{3,520\:\diagdown\!\!\!\!\!ft}{\diagdown\!\!\!\!\!min}*\frac{60\diagdown\!\!\!\!\!min}{hr}*\frac{1\:mi}{5,280\:\diagdown\!\!\!\!\!ft}=\frac{211,200\:mi}{5,280\:hrs}=40\:mi/hr[/tex]

Comparing both speeds,

[tex]\left[\begin{array}{ccc}White-tailed\:deer:&30\:mi/hr\\American\:bison:&40\:mi/hr\end{array}\right][/tex]

We can now say that the American bison is faster than the white-tailed deer by 10 miles per hour.

29. Why is the man stick like and the bison is rendered with such accurate detail?


Answers

The most probable reason for this is the lack of color availability and the lack of knowledge in human anatomy and proportions. Notice that even with details, the animals portrayed still lack in correct proportion and the other Elements of Art.

Explanation

In Art history, Art has steady progress in portraying human anatomy and proportions throughout the ages. It was in the Classical period that the Greeks observed proper proportions of the human anatomy, which is prominent in Greek sculptures. However, human anatomy in Greek paintings still lacked in the Form or Volume (Element of Art).
