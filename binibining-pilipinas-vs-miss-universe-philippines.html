12. differentiate universal culture vs particular culture :matinong sagot pls​


Answer:

Cultural particulars include the specific practices that distinguish cultures from one another. For example all people become hungry but the potential food sources defined as edible vary across cultures. That is what is appealing to eat in one society may be considered repulsive or simply unavailable in another.

Additionally, what is a cultural universal examples? Examples of elements that may be considered cultural universals are gender roles, the incest taboo, religious and healing ritual, mythology, marriage, language, art, dance, music, cooking, games, jokes, sports, birth and death because they involve some sort of ritual ceremonies accompanying them, etc.

Subsequently, question is, what is considered a cultural universal?

A cultural universal (also called an anthropological universal or human universal), as discussed by Emile Durkheim, George Murdock, Claude Lévi-Strauss, Donald Brown and others, is an element, pattern, trait, or institution that is common to all human cultures worldwide.

13. Differentiate the following terms: 1. Universal vs. Relative Truth 2. Public Truth vs. Private Truth 3. Empirical Truth vs. Rational Truth


Universal truth is something about all the facts about it, rather than things that are imagined. An example of a universal truth is science — laws that apply through out the entire universe is universal. Relative truth is truth about mere phenomena.  An example of a relative truth is something that only applies to you — Chinese food causes stomach aches.

Public truth is requiring the truth to be revealed to those who hold the right to be informed of it, with due regard for matters that are confidential or best not revealed for a good reason. An example of Public Truth: In victory, we accept the bestowal of meaning and elevated purpose. Private truth uses its theoretical argument to illuminate an array of puzzling social phenomena. An example of Private Truth: In defeat, we console ourselves that public loss is ultimately meaningless and impermanent.

Empirical truth states that experience and observation should be the means of gaining knowledge. On the other hand, Rational truth believes that opinions and actions should be based on reason rather than on religious beliefs or emotions. An example of empirical truth: Scientists formed a hypothesis to compare population growth of a species of bats vs the weather changes. Example of rational truth: A consumer with a goal to buy the most healthy muffin must know all the ingredients of the muffin, how each ingredient was cultivated and processed and the health impacts of all of this and be able to logically formulate this into an option of healthiness

26. as a university student, what is morality vs. Ethics ?​


Answer:

Ethics and morals relate to “right” and “wrong” conduct. While they are sometimes used interchangeably, they are different: ethics refer to rules provided by an external source, e.g., codes of conduct in workplaces or principles in religions. Morals refer to an individual's own principles regarding right and wrong.
