12. Learning Task 2 potential or kinetic energy1. a man running2. a dog in a 2-meter high bridge3. a man doing ice gice skating4. hanged bats5. motorcycle moving fast​


The man running has kinetic energy, which is the energy of motion. The energy is transferred from the chemical energy stored in the food he ate to the kinetic energy of his body as he moves.The dog on the 2-meter high bridge has potential energy, which is the energy an object has due to its position or state. In this case, the dog has gravitational potential energy because of its position above the ground.The man doing ice skating has kinetic energy, as he is moving on the ice. The energy comes from the chemical energy stored in the food he ate and is transferred to his muscles and then to kinetic energy as he moves across the ice.The hanged bats have potential energy, similar to the dog on the bridge. The bats have gravitational potential energy due to their position above the ground.The motorcycle moving fast has kinetic energy, which is the energy of motion. The energy comes from the combustion of fuel in the engine, which is converted to kinetic energy as the motorcycle moves forward.

14. 2. A motorcycle stunt man leaves the jump ramp traveling at 35 m/s at 22° above the horizontal. What are the horizontal and vertical components of his velocity?


Answer:

Horizontal = 3.45m/s ; Vertical = 13.11m/s

Explanation:

The horizontal component of the velocity is given by,

[tex]v_{x}[/tex] = v cos θ

Here, v is the speed and θ is the angle above the horizontal at which the stunt man leaves the ramp.

Substituting,

[tex]v_{x} = (35m/s)cos22degrees[/tex]

=32.45m/s

The vertical component of the velocity is given by,

[tex]v_{y}[/tex] = v sin θ

= [tex](35m/s) sin 22 degrees\\\\[/tex]

= 13.11m/s
