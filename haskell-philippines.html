14. Mat Weaving

All regions of the Philippines have a tradition of mat weaving, since mats are used all over the country as bedding material. It is primarily seen as a womans work.

Most mats are made out of leaves of pandan or the buri palm. They are often decorated with strips of one or two colors, creating simple linear patterns.

The process is as follows: one gathers the raw materials from plants, then strips, boils, dyes and weaves it.

The quality of a mat is judged from the softness of its material, the fineness of its weave, and the beauty of its design patterns.

Used for barter as well as bedding.

8. make a research on the early practitioners of basket weaving in the Philippines and their significant contributions to the present basket weaving industry​


Answer:

Basket weaving in the Philippines is one of the rural crafts in the country. The baskets are made from different materials including bamboo and rattan. Filipinos use baskets for storage of food and materials that they need to transport from one place to another. Some of the practitioners of basket weaving in the country are the women in Ilocos, specifically from the area of Paoay and Vigan, the women of the Tagbanwa tribe, and the Banayakeo Basket Weavers.

Explanation:

The women of Ilocos teach their children at an early age basket weaving skills. They teach them the process of connecting strands of materials to create a whole object forming a basket. Before, they sell it at the Banaue public market, now most of their crafts are reaching different parts of the country and even international because of their quality .

The women of the Tagbanwa Tribe made weaving baskets one of their main sources of income. The product that they use is available in most regions of Coron. While weavers' efficiency and market share grow, the women of Tagbanwa increasingly recognize the necessity of conserving pandan's raw material, as it is the main product that they use in basket weaving.

Basketry is so important to Banayakeo's economy that the city authority eve provided a facility for the association's meeting and training. They made a huge impact to even caught the attention of the NGO. Trainings were done with certificates. They also teach to new groups of younger age the skills weaving as it is very crucial for the region's means of revenue.
