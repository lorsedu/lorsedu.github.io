15. how can you describe a Filipino tradition paksiw ​


Answer:

Paksiw is a term associated to a dish that is cooked with vinegar and garlic. Lechon Paksiw is a Filipino pork dish made from leftover roast pig which is known as “Lechon”. Aside from using roast pig, leftover Lechon kawali can also be used. Making this dish is a practical way to recycle leftover pork.

21. ano sa English ang paksiw​


Answer:

whose name means "to cook and simmer in vinegar."

Explanation:

Filipino style of cooking. Common dishes bearing the term, however, can vary substantially depending on what is being cooked.

Sagot:

Ang english ng paksiw ay maaaring isalin sa "Vinegar Stew".

Paliwanag:

Ang paksiw at ang sinigang ay parehong "Stew" ang kahulugan sa english. Ngunit, isa sa pagkakaiba ng dalawang putahe na ito ay sa ginagamit nilang sangkap sa pagluluto. Ang sinigang ay gumagamit ng prutas tulad ng sampalok, kamias, kamatis, at batuan bilang pampa-asim na sangkap habang ang paksiw naman ay suka ang ginagamit.

Ilan sa mga halimbawa nito ay ang Sinigang na baboy sa Sampalok, Lechon Paksiw, Sinigang na Salmon sa Batuan, at Paksiw na Isda. Ang Sinigang ay masmarami ang sabaw kumpara sa Paksiw. Ito rin ang masgustong ulam ng mga Pilipino.

24. give some of description of paksiw na isda​


Answer:

Paksiw na Isda is a type of Filipino dish where fish is poached in vinegar seasoned with fish sauce and finger chillies. The name of the dish is derived from the word “paksiw” which is a cooking method where meat and seafood is simmered in vinegar hence you will see this term not only used on fish but also pork.
