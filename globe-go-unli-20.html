10. not all lines of longitude are shown on a globe if you want to find 20°W where should you look?


Answer:

If not all lines of longitude are shown on a globe, finding a specific longitude may be a bit tricky. However, one way to locate a specific longitude is to use the information provided on the globe to estimate where it might be.

Explanation:

Since the Earth is divided into 360 degrees of longitude, and each degree is further divided into 60 minutes, we can estimate the location of 20°W by dividing the globe into equal parts.

For example, if the globe shows every 30 degrees of longitude, we can estimate that 20°W would be located halfway between 0° and 30°W. Similarly, if the globe shows every 15 degrees of longitude, we can estimate that 20°W would be located between 15°W and 30°W, slightly closer to 15°W.

However, it is important to note that this method is not always accurate and may only provide an estimate of the location. For more precise measurements, a map or a more detailed globe may be needed.
