[tex]\purple{⊱┈────────────────────┈⊰}[/tex]

PROBLEM :

Sam and Alfie are brothers. After college graduation and being finally able to get a good they plan for retirement as follows. Starting at age 24, Sam deposits P15,000 at the end of each year for 30 years. Starting at age 42, Alfie deposits P30,000 at the end of each year for 20 years.

===========================================

ASKED :

Who will have the greater amount at retirement if both annuities earn 12% per year compounds annually?

===========================================

GIVEN :

Sam deposits Php 15,000 at the end of each year for 30 years.

Alfie deposits Php 30,000 at the end of each year for 20 years.

===========================================

FORMULA :

The formula of getting the future amount of compound interest is

[tex] \tt \green{F = P+P(1+r/n)^{nt}}[/tex]

Where,

F = future amountP = principalR = rateT = timeN = number of times compounded in a year

===========================================

[tex] \boxed{ \bold{ \: solution \: : \red{sam}\: }} \\ \\\tt \: F = 15,000 + \: 15,000(1+0.12/1)^1 {}^{(30)} \\ \tt \: F = 15,000+15,000(1+0.12)^3 {}^{0} \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \\ \tt \: F = 15,000+15,000(1.12)^3 {}^{0} \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \\ \tt\:F = 15,000+15,000(29.95992212) \: \: \: \\ \tt\:F = 15,000+449,398.8318 \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \\ { \underline{ \tt \green{F = 464,398.8318}}} \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: [/tex]

[tex]\boxed{ \bold{ \: solution \: : \red{alfie}\: }} \\ \\ \tt\: F = 30,000+30,000(1+0.12/1)^1 {}^{(20)} \\ \tt \:F = 30,000+30,000(1+0.12) {}^{20} \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \\ \tt \:F = 30,000+30,000(1.12)^2 {}^{0} \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \\ \tt \:F = 30,000+30,000(9.646293093) \: \: \: \: \\ \tt \:F = 30,000+289388.7928 \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \\ { \underline{ \tt \green{F = 309388.7928}}} \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: [/tex]

19. .Alfie is 2 years younger than John.John is 5 years older than Sally. If the sum of their ages is 44 years, how old is Sally?


Answer:

Sally is 12 years old.

Step-by-step explanation:

Alfie is 2 years younger than John. John is 5 years older than Sally. If the sum of their ages is 44 years, how old is Sally?

Let A be Alfie's age. J for John's. And S for Sally.

__________________________

Alfie is 2 years younger than John:

A = J - 2 <<<<< 1st equation

John is 5 years older than Sally:

S = J - 5 <<<<<< 2nd equation

The sum of their ages is 44 years:

J + A + S = 44 <<<<< 3rd equation

____________________

Substitute 1st and 2nd equation to the 3rd Equation to find John's age:

J + (J-2) + (J-5) = 44

J + J - 2 + J - 5 = 44

J + J + J - 2 - 5 = 44

3J - 7 = 44

3J = 44 + 7

3J = 51

3J/3 = 51/3

J = 17

John is 17 years old.

________________________

Solve for Alfie's age:

A = 17 - 2

A = 15

Alfie is 15 years old.

______________________

Solve for Sally's age:

S = 17 - 5

S = 12

Sally is 12 years old.

___________________

Check to verify the answer using the 3rd equation:

17 + 15 + 12 = 44

32 + 12 = 44

44 = 44
