3. Ano ang herrera law? Ano Ang nakapaloob dito


Ipinasa ang Herrera Law o Republic Act 6715 noong Marso 2, 1989. Naging instrumento ang batas na ito para payagan ang kontraktuwal sa paggawa. Sa ganitong kalakaran, ipinapaloob lamang sa kontrata ang isang empleyado nang hindi lalampas sa anim na buwan. Base sa batas, ang isang manggagawa o empleyado na lampas sa anim na buwan ay maituturing nang regular.

Mas marami na ngayon ang kontraktuwal kesa sa mga regular na manggagawa sa parehong publiko at pribadong sektor. Sa kontraktuwalisasyon na umiiral, maliban sa kawalan ng seguridad sa trabaho, mas mababa ang natatanggap na sahod kesa regular, walang mga benepisyo, at inaalisan ng karapatang mag-unyon.

Answer:

Cayetano received PhP 15,000. Herrera received PhP 25,000.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let us assign Herrera and Cayetano to algebraic expressions:

Cayetano = xHerrera = 2x-5,000

Solution:

x + (2x - 5,000) = 40,000 We have to separate the x variables so we need to add 5,000 to the other side (to 40,000) x + 2x = 40,000 + 5,000 3x = 45,000 x = 45,000 / 3 x = 15,000

Therefore, Cayetano received PhP 15,000. Since Herrera receives 5,000 less than twice Cayetano's:

(15,000 x 2) - 5,00030,000 - 5,000 = 25,000

Therefore, Herrera received PhP 25,000.

Check:

Paper Reaction:  "Dulo't Simula" by Zuela Herrera

Line, rhyme, a rhyming scheme, rhythm, and figurative language are all present in Zuela Herrera's "Dulo't Simula," as are other elements of poetry. If I go on to describe each and every instance of an Element in the poem, it may take quite some time. But allow me to give a few illustrations of each type.

The first thing to note about Line is that, while the poem does, in fact, have a line, unlike the Juan Miguel Severo poem (and predecessor to this one) that we have been analyzing, "Inggit," there are no stanzas. The continuity stems from the fact that both forms are Spoken Poetry. However, not all forms of spoken poetry use stanzas.

Now comes the rhyming portion. The poem has numerous rhyming couplets, such as the following:

Despite this, "palaging inaabangan ang isang hiwalayan, bahagharing hindi sapat na malaman ang pinagmulan, pilit hinahanap ang dulo at katapusan."

The words at the conclusion of each line should be noted. Considering how similarly each line ends in -an, we decided to call it "End Rhyme" since the rhyme occurs at the end of the lines.

The Rhyme Scheme comes next. Reading and analyzing the poem further has revealed to me that there is no predetermined rhyme pattern at work here. It evolves throughout the poem.

The Rhythm follows. The poem's flow and lyricism come from its use of stressed and unstressed words. This is preferable to just read or speaking the poetry out in front of an audience since it draws attention to the poem and conveys the speaker's genuine emotions to the listeners.

Finally, I'd want to talk about figurative language. Assonance, metaphor, simile, symbolism, personification, hyperbole, and onomatopoeia are all examples of Figurative Language found in this poetry.

The one line I particularly enjoyed, though I cannot claim that it is the best in the piece, is:

29. the label of the 200 ml rubbing a cold that mrs herrera but shows that it contains 40% ethyl alcohol how many percent is water​


Given:

percent by volume = 40%

volume of solution = 200 mL

Required:

volume of solvent

Equation:

[tex]\text{volume of solvent} = \dfrac{100\% - \text{percent by volume}}{100\%} \times \text{volume of solution}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\text{volume of solvent} = \dfrac{100\% - 40\%}{100\%} \times \text{200 mL}[/tex]

Answer:

volume of solvent = 120 mL
