20. art man newest maan of expression​


Answer:

The view that “art is imitation (representation)” has not only been challenged, it has been moribund in at least some of the arts since the 19th century. It was subsequently replaced by the theory that art is expression. Instead of reflecting states of the external world, art is held to reflect the inner state of the artist. This, at least, seems to be implicit in the core meaning of expression: the outer manifestation of an inner state. Art as a representation of outer existence (admittedly “seen through a temperament”) has been replaced by art as an expression of humans’ inner life.

30. Newest process of Reproduction (without Mating)​


"Please tap 'thanks' if my answer is correct. If my answer is wrong, please comment with the correct answer. Thank you!"

Parthenogenesis is one of the latest ways of reproduction without mating, which involves embryo development without fertilization from a male gamete. While parthenogenesis occurs naturally in some animals such as amphibians, reptiles, and insects, scientists have been able to initiate it in mammals through different techniques. Parthenogenesis involves activating the female gamete, typically an egg, to begin developing without being fertilized by a male gamete. The resulting embryo grows similarly to a fertilized one and is genetically identical to the mother. Parthenogenesis has several potential applications in medicine, agriculture, and conservation, but there are still challenges and ethical considerations, including genetic defects and ethical implications. Therefore, more research is required to explore its potential advantages and limitations.
