9. draw the location of the epicenter of an earthquake in Carmona, Silang, GMA Cavite if the hypothetical distance of epicenter in Carmona is 300 Km, Silang is 200 lkm, and GMA is 200km​ paturo po^_^.​


This method is used by geologists and seismologists called the triangulation method to determine the location of the epicenter. For the triangulation method to work we need three locations along with their hypothetical distance from the epicenter.

You will need:

A compassA pencilDifferent colored pensA copy of the map

Steps in doing the triangulation method:

From the map find the location labeled Carmona.this is where you will put the needle of your compass. Draw at least three circles of increasing size. A pencil would do but I recommend using a different colored pen for each location.Repeat steps 1 to 3 for the location Silang and GMA.

Note: Since there is no numbered scale provided, we will draw different sizes of circles but regarding the measurement of the circles, Silang and GMA’s circles must be roughly the same size and Carmona must have the biggest circle of them all. This is because of the distance provided from the epicenter.

When you’re done drawing the circles, there should be a point in the circles where all three different circles centered in three different locations would intersect. The point where they intersect is the epicenter of the earthquake.

27. What are the implications of Cavite Mutiny in today’s admiration of the word “freedom” in the Philippines?


Answer:

CAVITE MUTINY

Cavite Mutiny was the brief uprising of 200 Filipino troops and workers at the Cavite weaponry which prompted Spanish repression of the embryonic Philippine nationalist movement. Ironically, the harsh reaction of the Spanish authorities served ultimately to promote the nationalist cause. The mutiny gave birth to the causes of GomBurZa, which later on inspired Jose Rizal and the KKK. The mutiny became a domino that led to countless revolutionary movements through arms and pens. The mutiny was quickly crushed, but the Spanish regime under the reactionary governor Rafael de Izquierdo magnified the incident and used it as an excuse to clamp down on those Filipinos who had been calling for governmental reform. A number of Filipino intellectuals were seized and accused of complicity with the mutineers. After a brief trial, three priests—José Burgos, Jacinto Zamora, and Mariano Gómez—were publicly executed. The three subsequently became martyrs to the cause of Philippine independence.

The Cavite Mutiny, which occurred in 1872, was a significant event in Philippine history. It was the first major uprising against Spanish colonial rule, and it paved the way for the Philippine Revolution and the eventual independence of the Philippines. If the Cavite Mutiny had not happened, it is likely that the timeline of Philippine history would have been very different.

Without the Cavite Mutiny, it is possible that the Philippine Revolution may have been delayed or may not have happened at all. This could have led to a continuation of Spanish colonial rule in the Philippines for an extended period of time, potentially for several more decades. This would have had significant consequences for the lives of people living in the Philippines during the Spanish era, as they would have continued to be subject to the oppressive policies and practices of the colonial government.

Today, the effects of the Cavite Mutiny would still be felt in the lives of people in the Philippines. Without the Cavite Mutiny, the Philippines may not have gained independence as early as it did, and the country may have developed differently. This could have had an impact on the political, social, and economic landscape of the country, and on the lives of its citizens.
