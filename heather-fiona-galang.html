5. Devi, Jodhi and Fiona have saved some allowance to buy a gift for their father. The ratio of Devi's to Jodhi's savings is 1 : 3. The ratio of Jodhi's to Fiona's savings is 5 : 3. If Devi has saved $80, how much has Fiona saved?


Given:

The ratio of Devi's to Jodhi's savings is 1 : 3
The ratio of Jodhi's to Fiona's savings is 5 : 3
Devi has saved $80

Question:

How much has Fiona saved?

Solution:

1:3 and 1=$80 so replace 1 with the given value $80
and it becomes $80:3 after you replace the given value, multiply them with each other (we multiply it because 1 is less than 3) $80x3=$240 and $240 is Jodhi's savings so the the ratio of Devi's to Jodhi's savings will become $80:$240

5:3 and 5=$240 so replace 5 with the value that we solved which is $240 and it becomes $240:3 after you replace the value, divide it with the 3 (we divide it because 5 is greater than 3) $240/3=$80 and $80 is Fiona's savings so the ratio of Jodhi's to Fiona's savings will become $240:$80

Answer:

Fiona's savings is $80, the same as Jodhi's savings

7. On her first anatomy and physiology exam, Heather defined homeostasis as "the condition in which the body approaches room temperature and stays there." Do you agree with Heather’s definition?


Answer:

I do not agree with Heather’s definition of homeostasis because, by definition, homeostasis is defined as the ability of a body to create comfortable conditions by being at equilibrium or stabilizing itself internally whenever there are changes externally/outside the body. With regard to homeostasis, the room temperature of the internal part of the body, as with many other things in the body, is dynamic and does not stay at one place when external conditions change—it changes in order to maintain balance or equilibrium; hence, I do not agree with Heather’s definition of homeostasis.
