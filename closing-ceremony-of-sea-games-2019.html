Answer:

MANILA, Philippines – Thailand continued its dominant stretch in the 2019 Southeast Asian Games but not before enduring a late scare against a feisty Philippines side, 25-16, 25-22, 34-32, in women’s volleyball action at the PhilSports Arena on Thursday, December 5.

The Filipina spikers – already eliminated from gold medal contention just minutes prior to tip-off by way of Vietnam’s five-set win over Indonesia – still faced the Thai dynasty ready for a fight.

From a 19-24 deficit in the 3rd set, the Filipinas staged a blistering late 5-0 surge, forced multiple deadlocks, before eventually falling to the crushing might of the Thais in the extended set.

Thailand finally wrapped up the match after a Pimpichaya Kokram kill and a Wilavan Apinyapong finishing blow for a 34-32 win.

Alyssa Valdez led the late charge with 14 points on 12 attacks while SEA Games first-timer Mylene Paat added 11 markers.

Aby Maraño and Majoy Baron each chipped in 7 points while Jia Morado tallied 14 excellent sets.

Kokram led all scorers with 19 points while Apinyapong added 15 markers.

Nootsara Tomkom, meanwhile, steered the team to victory with a staggering 24 excellent sets in the three-set win.

While the Thais erected a massive 21-9 lead early after a Valdez attack error, fellow national team veteran Maraño sparked a spirited 6-1 run to cut the deficit to 7, 15-22.

However, the 11-time defending SEA Games champions promptly closed out the set, 25-16.

The Philippines then fed off Maraño’s fire and actually led 13-10 in the 2nd set after consecutive points by Baron.

The Filipinas went blow-by-blow and just trailed by one, 20-21, before the Thais again ran away with the frame, 25-22.

Thailand led by 5, 16-11, heading to the 2nd technical timeout, but had to go through a jittery stretch from a late Philippine rally in the extended set.

With the win, the Thais march on to their 15th straight gold medal match against Vietnam.

The Philippines will wrap up its elimination campaign versus Indonesia on Saturday in what will be a preview of the battle for bronze. – Rappler.com

13. Sanaysay tungkol sa sea games 2019


SEA GAMES 2019

Ang SEA GAMES o South East Asian Games ay binigyang tema na "We win as One". Binibigyang karangalan nito ang mga atleta sa ating bansa- ang Pilipinas pati na ang iba pang bansa na kalahok dito. Ginaganap ang pang-palakasan na ito bawat ikalawang taon na sinasalihan ng mga bansa na mula sa Timog-Silangang Asya (brainly.ph/question/161951). Ang International Olympic Committee (IOC) at ang Olympic Council of Asia ang nagbibigay ng mga regulasyon sa naturang palaro.

Kasaysayan ng SEA GAMES

Kilala ang SEA GAMES noon bilang Southeast Asian Peninsular Games. Kabilang sa SEAP Games ang mga bansang  Thailand, Myanmar, Malaysia, Laos, Timog Vietnam, Cambodia,at  Singapore. Ang unang SEAP Games ay naganap sa Bangkok noong Disyembre 1959 na nilahukan ng 527 atleta. Noong 1957, napagdesisyunan na isama sa SEAP Games ang Pilipinas at Indonesia.  

Ang ika-30th SEa GAMES

Ang SEA Games or ang Southeast Asian Games ay isang multi-sport na event na ginaganap ngayon dito sa Pilipinas, simula Nobyembre 30 hanggang Disyembre 11, 2019. Ito rin ang ang pang apat na beses na naganap dito sa Pilipinas ang naturang multi-sport event. Mayroon labing isang bansa na kasama sa dito, ito ang bansang Brunei, Cambodia, Indonesia, Laos, Malaysia, Myanmar, Pilipinas, Singapore, Thailand, Timor-Leste at Vietnam.

Tinalagang PHISGOC ( Philippine Southeast Asian Games Organizing Committee ) chairman si House Speaker Allan Peter Cayetano. Ayon sa kanya, ang budget ng naturingang multi sport event ay humigit kumulang na nasa P7.5 Bilyong piso, ang P6 bilyon ay galing sa budget ng gobyerno at ang iba ay galing sa sponsorship agreements, at ito ay aprubado ng senado at ng Pangulong Duterte.

Dineklara din ng Pangulong Duterte nitong nakaraan na libre na ang mga ticket sa SEA Games para masuportahan din ang mga atletang pinoy na manlalaro.

Ang SEA Games ay binubuo ng mga sports o laro tulad ng:

Basketball Archery Arnis Badminton Baseball Cycling Dance Sport Billiards Indoor Volleyball Squash Speed Skating Figure Skating BowlingWeightlifting Football (Men's) Tennis Soft tennis Gymnastics (Aerobic, Artistic, Rhythmic) Obstacle Course Boxing Kickboxing Ice hockey  Fencing Karatedo Wushu Cycling (Track) 3x3 Basketball E-Sports

Noong unag araw ng pagbubukas ng SEA Games, ito ay ginanap sa Philippine Arena at nilahukan ng mga kasamang bansa dito, ngunit Pilipinas ang umangat sa unang araw dahil sa suporta din ng mga kababayang Pinoy. Pinakita sa pagbubukas ng SEA Games ang ibat ibang katutubong sayaw dito sa Pilipinas, mga sikat na manlalaro hindi lang sa SEA Games kundi sa buong mundo. Si Ms. Universe 2015 Pia Wurtzbach (brainly.ph/question/1003003) ang Muse ng Pilipinas at ang pang huli ay si Senator Manny Pacquiao (brainly.ph/question/2510131) na nanguna sa pagsindi ng torch.

Pagraranggo sa unang tatlong araw Pilipinas na may 47 gold medalya, 31 silver,  at 17 bronze na ang total ay 95Malaysia na may 18 gold medalya Indonesia na may 12 gold medalya  Singapore at Thailand na may tag 8 gold medalya  Myanmar, Brunei at Cambodia na may tag 1 gold medal Timor Leste at Laos na wala pang nangunguha na kahit isa pang gold medal.

Reflection/ reaction about 2019 SEA Games

Hosting a big sporting event like the 2019 Southeast Asian Games will really be confronted by challenges. However, falling short of people’s expectations is tough in the digital age, because everything is magnified and netizens’ view can be shaped so easily by what the media is reporting.

I followed the whole event and I witnessed the controversies, the inspiring stories, and all in between. Weeks and days before the opening, there were still unfinished construction of the venues. Adding to it is the allegation of massive overspending especially of 50 million peso cauldron which was used for the ceremonial lighting of the SEA Games torch. If this is true, I will really feel disappointed because a lot of sports in our country do not have enough government funding to buy proper sports equipment or support to compete in various tournaments.

There are parts of the event that I am not proud of, but there are more parts where I am proud. I believe, media outlets should have highlighted the “good” instead of the “bad” and the “negative” especially before the opening ceremony and the early parts of SEA Games. Sadly, some media outlets went far by reporting erroneous details of the event such as the food that was served to the participants as well the location of the media press conference which both have been verified to be untrue.

Overall, I love the way SEA Games honored the past and present Filipino athletes. The opening and closing ceremonies were world-class and I really enjoyed the support of our artists in the music and arts industries.

In the end, we get our goal which is to finish victorious in the medal tally in the 30th SEA Games.  That, I believe is when #WeWinAsOne.
