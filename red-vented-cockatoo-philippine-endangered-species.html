1. why is the red vented cockatoo endangered?


The red-vented cockatoo (Cacatua haematuropygia) sometimes called the Philippine cockatoo or kalangay, is a critically endangered species of cockatoo that is endemic to the Philippines. It is roughly the size and shape of the Tanimbar corella, but is easily distinguished by thered feathers around the vent.

18. cite two examples of endangered and threatened species in the philippines​


In Bohol, tarsiers are used as a tourism symbol, although there are little regulations in place. At roadside attractions, many tarsiers are kept in appalling conditions. Because tarsiers are difficult to maintain alive in captivity, mortality is high; when they die, replacements are gathered from the wild. Wild tarsiers are also threatened by deforestation and more typhoons as a result of climate change.
