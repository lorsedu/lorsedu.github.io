The number of bleacher tickets sold was three times the number of th lower box tickets sold. If the total amount collected was 2150000, how many of each type of ticket were sold?


ANSWER:

The number of tickets sold per seat type:
Lower box = 1,000 tickets
Bleacher = 3,000 tickets
Upper box = 4,000 tickets

------------------------------- 
SOLUTION:
Total: 8,000 tickets.
Number of tickets per seat type:
Lower box = x
Bleacher = 3x
Upper Box = y  or 8,000 - 4x

               x + 3x + y = 8,000
               4x + y = 8,000
               y = 8,000 - 4x

Ticket prices:
Lower box: 500 (x)  or 500x
Bleacher: 150 (3x) or 450x
Upper: 300 (8,000 - 4x) or 2,400,000-1,200x

Equation:

500x + 450x + 2,400,000 - 1200x = 2,150,00
950x - 1,200x = 2,150.000
- 250x = -250,000
-250x/-250 = -250,000/-250
x = 1,000

Substitute 1,000 to x in:
y = 8,000 - 4 (1,000)
y = 8,000 - 4,000
y = 4,000
 
Substitute values of x and y to number of tickets:
Lower box: x                       = 1,000 tickets
Bleacher: 3x = 3 (1,000)     = 3,000 tickets
Upper box: y                       = 4, 000 tickets

Ticket Prices:

Bleacher: 150 (3,000)           =    450,000

20. In a survey of 100 students it is shown that 40 students have season football tickets, 30 students have basketball season tickets, and 20 students have both football and basketball season tickets. How many students have NEITHER basketball NOR football season tickets?


Answer:

50 students

Step-by-step explanation:

First add the total amount of students who have season and basketball tickets (I DONT mean BOTH, just who HAS.) That is 40 + 30 = 70

Then we subtract who have BOTH tickets, 70 - 20 = 50

Then subtract from the total number of students, 100 - 50 = 50 students.
