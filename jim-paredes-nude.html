10. £940 is divided between Gavin, Jim & Krutika so that Gavin gets twice as much as Jim, and Jim gets three times as much as Krutika. How much does Jim get?


Answer:

£282

Step-by-step explanation:

Let us represent each amount with the following variables:

g = amount of money Gavin gets

j = amount of money Jim gets

k = amount of money Krutika gets

The expression we have so far is

[tex]940 = g + j + k[/tex]

Gavin gets twice as much as Jim. Using our variables, we have

[tex]g = 2j[/tex]

Jim gets three times as much as Krutika. Using our variables, we have

[tex]j = 3k[/tex]

We can substitute the value of j to the second equation to see how Gavin and Krutika's money are related

[tex]g = 2j\\j = 3k\\\\g = 2(3k)\\g = 6k[/tex]

So, we can say that Gavin gets 6 times as much as Krutika has.

Since we expressed j and g in terms of k, we can substitute those values from the original equation.

[tex]g+k+j = 940\\g = 6k\\j = 3k\\\\6k + k + 3k = 940 \\10k = 940\\k = 94\\\\[/tex]

So Krutika has £94.

To find out how much the Gavin and Jim have, we substitute k =94 to their respective equations.

[tex]k = 94\\\\g = 6k \\g = 6(94)\\g = 564\\\\j = 3k\\j = 3(94)\\j = 282[/tex]

Jim has £282 while Gavin has £564.

In the second stanza, the speaker goes further, expressing a desire to be "naked of skin." They want to be truly vulnerable and open, without any protective barriers. This vulnerability is a prerequisite for true intimacy and connection with others.

In the final stanza, the speaker takes their desire to be nude to its logical conclusion: they want to be "naked of bone." This is the most extreme expression of the speaker's desire to be free and authentic. By shedding even their physical skeleton, the speaker is expressing a desire to be completely unencumbered, to exist in a state of pure being.

Overall, "In my desire to be nude" is a powerful exploration of the human desire for authenticity and freedom. The poem's rich imagery and metaphors invite the reader to consider their own desires for connection and vulnerability, and to contemplate the barriers that stand in the way of achieving these goals.
