29. what is the moral lesson of the story eveline by james joyce?


MORAL LESSON IN THE STORY OF EVELINE BY JAMES JOYCE

Memory, responsibility, decisions, conflict, escape, guilt, paralysis, and letting go are all themes in James Joyce's Eveline (or rather the inability to let go). This is significant since Eveline's life appears to have been happier or easier when her mother was alive. Eveline is about a nineteen-year-old Irish woman who wants to flee her abusive father and impoverished life in Ireland in order to find a new, better life for herself and her partner Frank in Buenos Aires. Paralysis, Epiphany, Stream of Consciousness, and Irish Social Conditions and Emigration are the key topics in Eveline. The word "paralysis" emphasizes the sensation of stagnation or paralysis that Joyce is conveying. The story "Eveline" is a great illustration of this.
